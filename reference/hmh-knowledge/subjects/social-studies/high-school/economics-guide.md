# High School Economics Guide
**Course Level:** Typically Grade 12 (Economics), Grade 11-12 (AP Macro/Microeconomics)
**Applies To:** All high school Economics, AP Macroeconomics, AP Microeconomics courses

---

## Overview

Economics examines how individuals, businesses, and governments make choices about scarce resources. Students analyze markets, economic systems, fiscal and monetary policy, and personal finance.

**College/Career Readiness:** Economics is essential for business, finance, public policy, and informed citizenship in a market economy.

**State Requirements:** Economics is often required or part of combined Government/Economics course.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Economic Fundamentals

- Scarcity and choice
- Opportunity cost
- Trade-offs
- Production Possibilities Curve (PPC)
- Comparative and absolute advantage
- Economic systems (traditional, command, market, mixed)

### 2. Microeconomics (Individual and Firm Behavior)

**Supply and Demand:**
- Law of demand, law of supply
- Equilibrium price and quantity
- Shifts in supply/demand (determinants)
- Elasticity (price elasticity of demand/supply, income elasticity, cross-price elasticity)

**Market Structures:**
- Perfect competition
- Monopoly
- Monopolistic competition
- Oligopoly

**Production and Costs:**
- Factors of production (land, labor, capital, entrepreneurship)
- Marginal cost, marginal revenue
- Profit maximization
- Law of diminishing marginal returns

**Factor Markets:**
- Labor market (wages, employment)
- Capital market (interest rates)
- Derived demand

**Market Failures:**
- Externalities (positive, negative)
- Public goods
- Role of government (regulation, taxes, subsidies)

### 3. Macroeconomics (National and Global Economy)

**National Income and GDP:**
- Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
- Nominal vs. real GDP
- GDP deflator, CPI (Consumer Price Index)
- Economic growth

**Unemployment:**
- Types (frictional, structural, cyclical)
- Natural rate of unemployment
- Full employment

**Inflation:**
- CPI, inflation rate
- Causes (demand-pull, cost-push)
- Effects of inflation

**Fiscal Policy:**
- Government spending and taxation
- Budget deficits and surpluses
- National debt
- Automatic stabilizers

**Monetary Policy:**
- Federal Reserve (Fed)
- Money supply
- Interest rates
- Expansionary vs. contractionary policy
- Tools: open market operations, reserve requirement, discount rate

**Business Cycle:**
- Expansion, peak, recession, trough
- Economic indicators

**International Trade:**
- Comparative advantage
- Balance of trade, balance of payments
- Exchange rates
- Trade policies (tariffs, quotas, free trade agreements)

### 4. Personal Finance (Often in Regular Economics)

- Budgeting and saving
- Credit and debt
- Investing (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)
- Insurance and risk management
- Taxes
- Financial planning

## AP Economics Exam Format

**Multiple Choice:** Interpret data, graphs, economic scenarios
**Free Response:** Analyze scenarios, draw/interpret graphs, solve problems

**AP Macroeconomics:**
- National income, inflation, unemployment
- Fiscal and monetary policy
- International trade and finance

**AP Microeconomics:**
- Supply and demand, elasticity
- Production and costs
- Market structures
- Factor markets, market failures

## Quick Reference

**Every Economics Material Must:**
- Teach fundamental concepts (scarcity, opportunity cost, supply/demand)
- (Micro) Analyze market structures and firm behavior
- (Macro) Analyze fiscal and monetary policy
- Include personal finance (regular Economics)
- Use graphs and data analysis
- (AP) Align to AP Macro/Micro CED
- Connect to current economic issues

**Remember:** Economics is the study of choice. Understanding economics empowers students to make informed personal financial decisions and evaluate public policy.
