# High School Instructional Strategies (Grades 9-12)
**Applies To:** All high school instructional materials

---

## Overview

High school instruction requires age-appropriate strategies that build independence, critical thinking, and college/career readiness. Materials must support active learning, inquiry, and student agency.

---

## Key HS Instructional Strategies

### 1. Inquiry-Based Learning
- Student-driven questions and investigations
- Problem-Based Learning (PBL)
- Project-Based Learning
- Case studies and real-world problems

### 2. Socratic Seminars and Academic Discussions
- Text-based discussions
- Evidence-based argumentation
- Respectful disagreement and debate
- Build on others' ideas

### 3. Close Reading and Textual Analysis (All Subjects)
- Annotate complex texts
- Identify author's purpose, bias, perspective
- Analyze arguments and evidence
- Synthesize multiple sources

### 4. Research and Inquiry
- Develop research questions
- Evaluate source credibility
- Synthesize information from multiple sources
- Proper citation (MLA, APA, Chicago)

### 5. Collaborative Learning
- Group projects and investigations
- Peer review and feedback
- Jigsaw activities
- Think-Pair-Share (still effective in HS)

### 6. Differentiation for Diverse Learners
- Tiered assignments (on-level, honors, AP)
- Student choice in topics, texts, products
- Multiple entry points
- Scaffolding for struggling students, extensions for advanced

---

## College Readiness Strategies

**Time Management and Organization:**
- Long-term project planning
- Study skills and note-taking
- Self-advocacy and help-seeking

**Academic Writing:**
- Thesis-driven essays
- Research papers
- Literary analysis
- Argument with counterclaims

**Test Preparation:**
- Practice with standardized test formats (SAT, ACT, AP)
- Test-taking strategies
- Timed writing
