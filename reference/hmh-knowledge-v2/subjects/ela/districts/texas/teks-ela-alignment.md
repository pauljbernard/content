# TEKS ELA Alignment Guide
**Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills for English Language Arts**
**Source:** Texas Education Agency
**Applies To:** K-8 ELA (Texas only)

---

## Overview

**Texas uses TEKS (Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills)** for ELA content standards, just as it does for mathematics. All Texas ELA instructional materials must explicitly align to TEKS ELA standards.

**Core Principle:** Every lesson and activity must align to specific TEKS ELA standards with clear evidence of alignment.

---

## TEKS ELA vs. CCSS ELA

| Aspect | TEKS ELA (Texas) | CCSS ELA (California, etc.) |
|--------|------------------|----------------------------|
| **Standard Format** | {Grade}.{Strand}.{Standard} | CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.{Grade}.{Strand}.{Number} |
| **Example** | 5.6C | CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.5.3 |
| **Organization** | By strand and skill | By strand and standard number |
| **Phonics** | Explicit K-3 requirements | Less prescriptive |
| **Skills** | More granular | Broader standards |

---

## TEKS ELA Standard Format

### Structure:

**Format:** `{Grade}.{Strand}.{Standard Letter}`

**Example:** `5.6C`

**Breakdown:**
- `5` = Grade 5
- `6` = Reading Comprehension strand
- `C` = Standard C within strand

## TEKS ELA Strands by Grade

### K-8 Strands:

**Foundations (K-3 only):**
- **Strand 1:** Developing and Sustaining Foundational Language Skills
  - Print concepts
  - Phonological awareness
  - Phonics and word analysis
  - Fluency

**Reading Strands (K-8):**
- **Strand 6:** Comprehension Skills
- **Strand 7:** Response Skills
- **Strand 8:** Multiple Genres (Literary texts)
- **Strand 9:** Multiple Genres (Informational texts)
- **Strand 10:** Author's Purpose and Craft

**Writing Strands (K-8):**
- **Strand 11:** Composition (Writing Process)
- **Strand 12:** Genres (Narrative, Informational, Argumentative, Correspondence)

**Inquiry and Research (K-8):**
- **Strand 13:** Inquiry and Research

## Example Alignment (Grade 5 Inference):

**Standard:**
```
5.6C - Make inferences and use evidence to support understanding.
```

**Lesson Alignment:**
- **Read-Aloud:** Students listen to text and make predictions (inference practice)
- **Close Reading:** Students identify evidence in text that supports inferences
- **Discussion:** Students share inferences and cite textual evidence
- **Writing:** Students write inferences with evidence from text

**ELPS Addressed:**
- ELPS 4.G: Show comprehension through discussion and writing
- ELPS 3.E: Share inferences in cooperative learning
- ELPS 5.G: Write explanations of inferences with evidence

**Literacy Routines Used:**
- Close Reading Protocol (finding evidence)
- Think-Pair-Share (discussing inferences)
- Annotation Protocol (marking evidence)

## Key TEKS ELA Differences from Math

### Foundational Skills (K-3):
Unlike math, ELA has explicit phonics and foundational skills standards in K-3.

**Example:** `1.1B(iii) - Decode words with consonant blends`

**Implication:** K-3 ELA lessons must address phonics systematically.

---

### Genre Standards:
TEKS ELA specifies genre-specific standards for both literary and informational texts.

**Example:**
- `5.8A` - Infer themes in literary texts
- `5.9A` - Main idea in informational texts

**Implication:** Lessons must specify genre and align appropriately.

---

### Writing Genres:
TEKS ELA specifies four writing genres with separate standards:

**Genres:**
- Narrative
- Informational/Explanatory
- Argumentative
- Correspondence (letters, emails, etc.)

**Example:** `5.12A` - Compose argumentative texts with clear claim and reasons

## Alignment Validation Checklist

### Content Alignment:
- [ ] TEKS ELA standard code correct and complete
- [ ] Full standard text included or referenced
- [ ] All parts of standard addressed in lesson
- [ ] Genre specified if applicable (literary vs. informational)
- [ ] No out-of-grade content required

### ELPS Alignment:
- [ ] At least 1-2 ELPS standards explicitly connected
- [ ] Language domains addressed (listening, speaking, reading, writing)
- [ ] EB scaffolds provided at 3 proficiency levels

### Literacy Routines:
- [ ] At least 1 literacy routine embedded
- [ ] Routine aligns with TEKS focus
- [ ] Time allocated for routine

### Assessment:
- [ ] Assessment item aligns to TEKS
- [ ] DOK level appropriate
- [ ] Rubric provided for constructed response

## Texas Compliance for ELA

### Same Requirements as Math:

**Because Texas compliance is district-wide (cross-subject), ELA has identical requirements:**

1. **TEKS Alignment** - Must align to TEKS ELA (not CCSS)
2. **ELPS Alignment** - Must support EBs in all 4 domains
3. **IPACC Compliance** - Same 9 content restriction categories
4. **SBOE Quality** - Same 6 quality dimensions
5. **CEID Standards** - Same diversity requirements
6. **Digital-Print Parity** - Same 3 levels of parity
7. **Accessibility** - Same WCAG 2.1 AA requirements

**Benefit of Architecture:** ELA automatically inherits all Texas compliance knowledge without duplication!
