# High School World History Guide
**Course Level:** Typically Grade 9-10 (World History), Grade 10-12 (AP World History, AP European History)
**Applies To:** All high school World History, AP World History, AP European History courses

---

## Overview

World History examines global historical developments, interactions between societies, and continuity and change across time and space. Students analyze how human societies evolved, interacted, and shaped the modern world.

**AP World History: Modern:** Covers 1200 CE to present, emphasizing global interactions and comparisons
**AP European History:** Covers 1450 to present, emphasizing European developments and global impact

---

## Key Themes (AP World History)

1. **Humans and the Environment**
2. **Cultural Developments and Interactions**
3. **Governance**
4. **Economic Systems**
5. **Social Interactions and Organization**
6. **Technology and Innovation**

---

## Major Periods (AP World History: Modern)

1. **1200-1450:** Global Tapestry (trade routes, religions, empires)
2. **1450-1750:** Networks of Exchange (Columbian Exchange, maritime empires)
3. **1750-1900:** Revolutions (Industrial, political, imperialism)
4. **1900-Present:** Global Conflicts, decolonization, globalization

---

## Major Civilizations and Regions

**Early Civilizations:** Mesopotamia, Egypt, Indus Valley, China, Mesoamerica
**Classical:** Greece, Rome, Han China, Gupta India, Persian Empire
**Post-Classical:** Islamic Caliphates, Byzantine, Tang/Song China, Medieval Europe, Mongol Empire
**Early Modern:** Renaissance, Reformation, Scientific Revolution, Enlightenment, absolutism
**Modern:** Industrialization, imperialism, WWI/II, Cold War, decolonization, globalization

---

## AP European History Periods

1. **1450-1648:** Renaissance, Reformation, exploration
2. **1648-1815:** Absolutism, Enlightenment, French Revolution, Napoleon
3. **1815-1914:** Industrialization, nationalism, imperialism
4. **1914-Present:** World wars, totalitarianism, Cold War, European integration

---

## Historical Thinking Skills

- Developments and Processes
- Sourcing and Situation
- Claims and Evidence
- Contextualization
- Making Connections (Comparison, Causation, CCOT)
- Argumentation

## Quick Reference

**Every World History Material Must:**
- Cover major civilizations and regions
- Emphasize connections and comparisons across societies
- Develop historical thinking skills
- (AP) Align to AP World/European History CED
- Include diverse global perspectives
- Analyze continuity and change over time

**Remember:** World History reveals how interconnected our world has always been and how past global interactions shaped the modern world.
