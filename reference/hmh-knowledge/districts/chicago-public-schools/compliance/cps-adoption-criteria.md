# Chicago Public Schools Adoption Criteria
**District:** Chicago Public Schools (CPS)
**State:** Illinois  
**Students:** 350,000 (third largest US school district)
**Applies To:** All K-8 instructional materials

---

## Overview

**CPS** is the third largest school district in the US, serving 350,000 students across Chicago. CPS follows Illinois state standards (CCSS, NGSS) with significant district-specific requirements focused on equity, racial justice, and college readiness.

**Key Principle:** Materials must meet Illinois state standards AND CPS district priorities.

---

## Relationship to Illinois State

### State Standards (Base Requirements):

CPS follows all Illinois state standards:
- **Content Standards:** CCSS (Math and ELA), NGSS (Science), Illinois Social Science Standards
- **Language Standards:** WIDA English Language Development Standards
- **State Assessments:** Illinois Assessment of Readiness (IAR) for ELA/Math, Illinois Science Assessment (ISA)

**See:** `/districts/illinois/compliance/illinois-adoption-criteria.md` for state requirements
**See:** `/districts/illinois/language/illinois-el-standards.md` for WIDA framework

---

### CPS District Layer (Additional Requirements):

CPS adds significant district-specific requirements:

1. **Racial Equity and Social Justice:** Explicit anti-racist curriculum framework
2. **College and Career Readiness:** Strong focus on postsecondary preparation  
3. **Whole Child Development:** Social-emotional learning and wellness
4. **Personalized Learning:** Data-driven differentiation and student choice
5. **Chicago Identity:** Local context, history, and culture

---

## CPS Adoption Process

### 1. Content Area Task Force (Teacher-Led):
- Teachers, instructional coaches, specialists review materials
- Alignment to state standards and CPS priorities
- Equity audit (representation, bias, accessibility)
- Pilot recommendations

**Timeline:** 2-3 months

### 2. Pilot Implementation (20-40 schools):
- Diverse school sample (performance, demographics, regions)
- Teacher surveys and feedback
- Student achievement data
- Usability and engagement metrics

**Timeline:** 6-12 months

### 3. CPS Leadership Review:
- Academic officer review
- Equity and access review
- Budget and scalability analysis
- Community input sessions

**Timeline:** 2-3 months

### 4. Board Approval:
- Chicago Board of Education vote
- Public comment period
- Contract finalization

**Timeline:** 1-2 months

## CPS-Specific Requirements

### 1. Racial Equity and Social Justice Framework

**CPS Commitment:** All curriculum must advance racial equity and social justice

**Required Elements:**

**Diverse Representation:**
- Students of color prominently featured (CPS is 90%+ students of color)
- Diverse authors, scientists, mathematicians, historical figures
- Chicago's diversity reflected (African American 36%, Hispanic/Latino 47%, other 17%)

**Multiple Perspectives:**
- History taught from multiple viewpoints
- Counter-narratives to dominant stories
- Centering marginalized voices

**Critical Consciousness:**
- Students analyze systems of power and privilege
- Identify injustice and inequity
- Develop tools for advocacy and action

**Anti-Bias Content:**
- Materials free from stereotypes
- Culturally sustaining (not just responsive)
- Asset-based view of communities

**Examples:**
- Math: Word problems featuring Chicago neighborhoods, economic justice themes
- Science: Environmental justice, health disparities, STEM equity
- Social Studies: Chicago's civil rights history, Great Migration, labor movements
- ELA: Diverse authors, texts addressing social issues

---

### 2. College and Career Readiness (Star Framework)

**CPS STAR (Students Today Are Ready) Framework**

**Elementary (K-5):**
- Academic foundations (reading by 3rd grade, math proficiency)
- Growth mindset and learning skills
- Exposure to careers and postsecondary options

**Middle School (6-8):**
- Rigorous academics (Algebra in 8th grade)
- Career exploration and interest inventories
- High school preparation (course selection, study skills)
- College-going culture

**Materials Must:**
- Build toward college-level literacy and numeracy
- Connect content to careers and real-world applications
- Develop 21st-century skills (collaboration, critical thinking, etc.)
- Include college/career vocabulary and concepts

---

### 3. Whole Child Development (Social-Emotional Learning)

**CPS SEL (Social-Emotional Learning) Integration**

**CASEL Framework (5 Competencies):**
1. Self-Awareness
2. Self-Management
3. Social Awareness
4. Relationship Skills
5. Responsible Decision-Making

**In All Subjects:**
- Collaborative learning opportunities (relationship skills)
- Reflection and goal-setting (self-awareness, self-management)
- Perspective-taking and empathy (social awareness)
- Problem-solving with ethical reasoning (responsible decision-making)

**Materials Should:**
- Include SEL-integrated activities
- Provide opportunities for student voice and choice
- Foster positive classroom culture
- Support trauma-informed practices

---

### 4. Personalized Learning and Data Use

**CPS Focus:** Use data to differentiate and personalize instruction

**Required Features:**

**Formative Assessment:**
- Embedded throughout units
- Immediate feedback to students
- Data dashboards for teachers
- Progress monitoring tools

**Differentiation:**
- Tiered activities (below, on, above grade level)
- Flexible grouping guidance
- Intervention and extension resources
- Multiple pathways to mastery

**Student Choice:**
- Choice in topics, texts, projects (where appropriate)
- Voice in demonstrating learning
- Student goal-setting and self-assessment

**Data Integration:**
- Aligned to CPS district assessments (NWEA MAP, IAR)
- Analytics and reporting tools
- Action plans based on data

---

### 5. Chicago Identity and Local Context

**CPS Emphasis:** Connect to Chicago history, culture, communities

**Chicago Connections:**

**Social Studies:**
- Chicago history (Great Migration, civil rights, labor movements)
- Chicago geography (neighborhoods, Lake Michigan, regions)
- Chicago government and civics
- Current Chicago issues

**All Subjects:**
- Examples featuring Chicago neighborhoods
- References to local landmarks and culture
- Chicago-based case studies
- Field trip connections to Chicago institutions

**Cultural Responsiveness:**
- Reflect Chicago's diversity
- Honor neighborhood identities
- Connect to students' lived experiences
- Leverage community cultural wealth

---

## CPS Assessment and Accountability

### State Assessments (Illinois):
- IAR (Illinois Assessment of Readiness): Grades 3-8 ELA/Math
- ISA (Illinois Science Assessment): Grades 5 and 8
- WIDA ACCESS: English Learners

### CPS District Assessments:
- NWEA MAP (Measures of Academic Progress): 3x per year, grades K-8
- CPS-created common assessments
- Performance tasks

**Materials Must:**
- Align to IAR and ISA format/rigor
- Prepare students for NWEA MAP
- Include embedded formative and summative assessments
