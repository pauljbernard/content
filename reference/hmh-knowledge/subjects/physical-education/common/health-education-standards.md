# Health Education Standards
**Level:** K-12
**Applies To:** All health education programs

---

## Overview

Health education provides students with the knowledge and skills to adopt, practice, and maintain health-enhancing behaviors. Comprehensive health education addresses physical, mental, emotional, and social health.

**Key Principle:** "Health education empowers students to make informed, healthy choices throughout their lives."

---

## National Health Education Standards (8)

### Standard 1: Core Concepts
Students will comprehend concepts related to health promotion and disease prevention.

**Topics by Grade Band:**

**K-2:**
- Body systems basics (bones, muscles)
- Nutrition (food groups, healthy eating)
- Personal hygiene (handwashing, teeth brushing)
- Safety (traffic, fire, strangers)

**3-5:**
- Nutrition labels, balanced meals
- Communicable vs. non-communicable diseases
- Puberty basics (age-appropriate)
- Injury prevention

**6-8:**
- Body systems in depth
- Nutrition and chronic disease
- Puberty, reproductive health
- Mental health basics (stress, anxiety)
- Substance abuse prevention (tobacco, alcohol, drugs)
- Safety (internet, dating relationships)

**9-12:**
- Chronic diseases (heart disease, diabetes, cancer)
- Reproductive health, STI prevention
- Mental health (depression, suicide prevention)
- Substance abuse (opioids, vaping)
- Healthy relationships, consent
- Health careers

---

### Standard 2: Access Valid Information
Students will analyze the influence of family, peers, culture, media, technology on health behaviors.

**Skills:**
- Identify trusted sources (CDC, doctors, school nurse)
- Evaluate websites for credibility
- Recognize advertising tactics
- Understand peer and media influence

**Example:**
- Grade 4: Identify trusted adult to ask about health
- Grade 8: Evaluate health information on website (credibility check)
- HS: Analyze how media influences body image

---

### Standard 3: Access Resources
Students will demonstrate the ability to access valid information, products, and services.

**Skills:**
- Know where to find help (school counselor, hotlines, clinics)
- Locate reliable health information
- Access community resources

**Example:**
- Grade 5: Identify school/community health helpers
- Grade 8: Locate mental health resources
- HS: Access sexual health services, know insurance/clinic options

---

### Standard 4: Interpersonal Communication
Students will demonstrate the ability to use interpersonal communication skills to enhance health.

**Skills:**
- Assertive communication (I-statements)
- Active listening
- Refusal skills (saying no to peer pressure)
- Conflict resolution
- Asking for help

**Example:**
- Grade 3: Use I-statements to express feelings
- Grade 7: Demonstrate refusal skills for substance use
- HS: Communicate boundaries in relationships, consent

---

### Standard 5: Decision-Making
Students will demonstrate the ability to use decision-making skills to enhance health.

**Process:**
1. Identify the decision
2. Consider alternatives
3. Evaluate consequences (pros/cons, risks)
4. Make a choice
5. Reflect on outcome

**Example:**
- Grade 4: Decide what to eat for healthy snack
- Grade 8: Decide how to respond to peer pressure
- HS: Make informed decisions about sexual health

---

### Standard 6: Goal-Setting
Students will demonstrate the ability to use goal-setting skills to enhance health.

**SMART Goals:**
- Specific
- Measurable
- Achievable
- Relevant
- Time-bound

**Example:**
- Grade 5: Set goal to exercise 30 min, 3x per week
- Grade 8: Set goal to manage stress (practice deep breathing daily)
- HS: Set long-term health goal (maintain healthy weight, improve sleep)

---

### Standard 7: Self-Management
Students will demonstrate the ability to practice health-enhancing behaviors and avoid/reduce health risks.

**Skills:**
- Healthy habits (sleep, nutrition, exercise, hygiene)
- Stress management
- Avoiding risky behaviors
- Managing chronic conditions (asthma, diabetes)

**Example:**
- Grade 2: Practice handwashing routine
- Grade 7: Use stress management technique (deep breathing, exercise)
- HS: Practice safe driving, avoid substance use

---

### Standard 8: Advocacy
Students will demonstrate the ability to advocate for personal, family, and community health.

**Skills:**
- Encourage others to make healthy choices
- Promote health causes (anti-bullying, mental health awareness)
- Work for healthy school/community environment

**Example:**
- Grade 4: Encourage family member to eat vegetables
- Grade 8: Create anti-vaping campaign for school
- HS: Advocate for mental health resources, volunteer for health organization

## Comprehensive Health Education Topics

### 1. Nutrition and Physical Activity

**Concepts:**
- MyPlate (5 food groups)
- Balanced diet, portion control
- Reading nutrition labels
- Healthy vs. unhealthy fats, sugars
- Hydration
- Relationship between diet, exercise, and chronic disease

**Skills:**
- Plan balanced meals
- Make healthy food choices
- Set fitness goals

---

### 2. Growth, Development, and Sexual Health

**Elementary (K-5):**
- Body parts (correct anatomical terms)
- Body changes during growth
- Puberty basics (age-appropriate, often grade 4-5)
- Consent and body autonomy ("my body belongs to me")

**Middle School (6-8):**
- Puberty in depth (physical, emotional changes)
- Reproductive system anatomy
- Menstruation, hygiene
- Abstinence
- Healthy vs. unhealthy relationships

**High School (9-12):**
- Abstinence and contraception
- STI prevention, testing
- Pregnancy prevention
- Consent (ongoing, enthusiastic, can be withdrawn)
- Healthy relationships, dating violence prevention
- LGBTQ+ inclusive content

**Teaching Approach:**
- Medically accurate, age-appropriate
- Answer questions honestly
- Use correct terminology
- Inclusive (all orientations, gender identities)
- Respect family values while providing facts
- Comply with state laws (opt-out options, parent notification)

---

### 3. Mental and Emotional Health

**Topics:**
- Recognizing emotions
- Stress management (deep breathing, exercise, talking to trusted adult)
- Coping skills (healthy vs. unhealthy)
- Self-esteem, body image
- Depression, anxiety (signs, seeking help)
- Suicide prevention (warning signs, crisis hotlines)

**Skills:**
- Identify emotions
- Use coping strategies
- Seek help when needed
- Support peers in crisis

**Resources:**
- 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline
- School counselor
- Mental health hotlines (Crisis Text Line: text HOME to 741741)

---

### 4. Substance Abuse Prevention

**Topics:**
- Tobacco, vaping (nicotine addiction, health risks)
- Alcohol (short-term and long-term effects, impaired judgment)
- Marijuana, other drugs (legal issues, health effects)
- Prescription drug misuse
- Opioid crisis
- Refusal skills

**Approach:**
- Evidence-based (not scare tactics)
- Focus on immediate consequences relevant to teens (athletic performance, appearance, social effects) not just long-term disease
- Teach refusal skills
- Connect to decision-making

---

### 5. Safety and Injury Prevention

**Topics:**
- Personal safety (strangers, trusted adults, inappropriate touch)
- Internet safety (privacy, cyberbullying, online predators)
- Fire, water, traffic safety
- Sports safety (helmets, protective gear)
- First aid, CPR (HS)
- Dating violence, sexual assault prevention
- Distracted driving, seat belt use

---

### 6. Disease Prevention

**Concepts:**
- Communicable diseases (colds, flu, COVID-19) - how they spread, prevention
- Non-communicable diseases (heart disease, diabetes, cancer) - risk factors, prevention
- Immunizations
- Hygiene (handwashing, covering coughs)

---

### 7. Consumer Health

**Topics:**
- Evaluating health products and services
- Advertising tactics
- Health insurance basics
- Quackery and health fraud

---

## Sensitive Topics: Best Practices

### Trauma-Informed Approach

**Be aware:**
- Students may have experienced trauma (abuse, violence, loss)
- Topics like sexual health, substance abuse, mental health can trigger

**Strategies:**
- Provide content warnings
- Offer alternative assignments if needed
- Never force sharing of personal information
- Know resources (school counselor, hotlines)
- Mandatory reporting (if abuse disclosed)

---

### Parental Involvement

- Notify parents of sensitive topics (sexual health, mental health)
- Offer opt-out (per state law)
- Provide resources for home conversations
- Be available to answer parent questions

---

### Confidentiality and Mandatory Reporting

**Students may disclose:**
- Abuse or neglect (physical, sexual, emotional)
- Self-harm or suicidal ideation
- Harm to others

**Response:**
- Listen without judgment
- Affirm: "I'm glad you told me"
- Explain mandatory reporting: "I need to tell the school counselor/administrator to get you help"
- Follow school protocol immediately

## Assessment in Health Education

### Knowledge Assessment
- Tests, quizzes (concepts)
- Projects, presentations
- Exit tickets

### Skill Assessment
- Role-plays (refusal skills, communication)
- Decision-making scenarios
- Goal-setting plans with tracking

### Authentic Assessment
- Public service announcements (advocacy)
- Health behavior journals
- Community projects

## Quick Reference

**Every Health Education Lesson Should:**
- ✅ Align to National Health Education Standards
- ✅ Be age-appropriate and medically accurate
- ✅ Teach skills (communication, decision-making), not just concepts
- ✅ Be inclusive (all students, families, identities)
- ✅ Connect to students' lives (relevant topics)
- ✅ Provide resources (hotlines, websites, school supports)
- ✅ Be trauma-informed (sensitive topics handled with care)
- ✅ Empower students to make healthy choices

**Remember:** Health education can literally save lives. Teach with accuracy, compassion, and empowerment.
