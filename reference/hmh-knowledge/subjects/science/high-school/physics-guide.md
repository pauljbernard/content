# High School Physics Guide
**Course Level:** Typically Grade 11-12 (Physics), Grade 11-12 (AP Physics)
**Prerequisites:** Algebra II or concurrent enrollment (for regular Physics); Calculus or concurrent (for AP Physics C)
**Applies To:** All high school Physics and AP Physics courses (1, 2, C: Mechanics, C: E&M)

---

## Overview

Physics is the study of matter, energy, motion, and forces‚Äîthe fundamental laws governing the universe. High school physics introduces mechanics, energy, waves, electricity, and magnetism. AP Physics courses range from algebra-based (AP Physics 1 & 2) to calculus-based (AP Physics C: Mechanics and E&M).

**College/Career Readiness:** Physics is required for engineering, physics, astronomy, and many STEM majors. It develops quantitative problem-solving, mathematical modeling, and scientific reasoning.

**AP Physics Options:**
- **AP Physics 1:** Algebra-based, mechanics + waves + circuits (first semester college physics)
- **AP Physics 2:** Algebra-based, fluids + thermodynamics + E&M + modern physics (second semester)
- **AP Physics C: Mechanics:** Calculus-based mechanics
- **AP Physics C: Electricity and Magnetism (E&M):** Calculus-based E&M

## Core Physics Concepts

### 1. Kinematics (Motion)

**Key Concepts:**
- Position, displacement, distance
- Velocity (average, instantaneous)
- Acceleration
- Motion graphs (position-time, velocity-time, acceleration-time)
- Kinematic equations (constant acceleration)
- Projectile motion (2D kinematics)
- Circular motion (centripetal acceleration)

**AP Physics C:**
- Calculus-based (derivatives and integrals for velocity, acceleration)

---

### 2. Dynamics (Forces)

**Key Concepts:**
- Newton's Laws of Motion
  - 1st Law: Inertia (object at rest/motion stays unless force applied)
  - 2nd Law: F = ma (force causes acceleration)
  - 3rd Law: Action-reaction pairs
- Free body diagrams (FBD)
- Types of forces (gravity, normal, tension, friction, applied)
- Net force and equilibrium
- Friction (static, kinetic, coefficient of friction)

**AP Physics:**
- Advanced applications (inclined planes, connected objects, pulley systems)
- Calculus-based force analysis (AP Physics C)

---

### 9. Fluids (AP Physics 2)

**Key Concepts:**

---

### 10. Thermodynamics (AP Physics 2)

**Key Concepts:**
- Temperature and heat
- Specific heat, heat capacity
- Phase changes (latent heat)
- Laws of Thermodynamics (0th, 1st, 2nd)

---

### 11. Modern Physics (AP Physics 2)

**Key Concepts:**
- Atomic structure
- Quantum mechanics (photons, wave-particle duality, uncertainty principle)
- Photoelectric effect
- Nuclear physics (radioactivity, fission, fusion)

## Quick Reference

**Every HS Physics Material Must:**
- Align to NGSS HS-PS2/3/4 or state standards (TEKS, etc.)
- Emphasize inquiry and investigation (NGSS practices)
- Include hands-on labs and demonstrations
- Develop quantitative problem-solving skills (kinematics, dynamics, energy, circuits)
- Use visual representations (FBD, graphs, diagrams)
- Connect to real-world applications (engineering, technology, natural phenomena)
- Prepare for AP exam (if applicable: 1, 2, C: Mech, C: E&M)
- Support emergent bilinguals (vocabulary, math scaffolding)

**Remember:** Physics reveals the fundamental laws governing the universe‚Äîfrom subatomic particles to galaxies. Mastering physics develops powerful problem-solving skills and opens doors to engineering, technology, and scientific discovery.
