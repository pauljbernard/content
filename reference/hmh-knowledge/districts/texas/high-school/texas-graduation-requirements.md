# Texas High School Graduation Requirements
**State:** Texas
**Applies To:** All Texas high school graduation programs

---

## Overview

Texas offers multiple graduation programs: Foundation High School Program (minimum), Foundation + Endorsement, and Distinguished Level of Achievement. All programs require passing STAAR End-of-Course (EOC) exams.

---

## Foundation High School Program (Minimum - 22 Credits)

**English Language Arts:** 4 credits
- English I, II, III, IV

**Mathematics:** 3 credits (must include Algebra I)
- Algebra I (required)
- Geometry (strongly recommended)
- One additional math course

**Science:** 3 credits (must include Biology and IPC or other science)
- Biology (required)
- IPC (Integrated Physics and Chemistry) OR Chemistry OR Physics
- One additional science course

**Social Studies:** 3 credits
- World Geography or World History (1 credit)
- US History (1 credit, required)
- US Government (0.5 credit, required)
- Economics with emphasis on free enterprise (0.5 credit, required)

**Physical Education:** 1 credit

**Fine Arts:** 1 credit

**Languages Other Than English (LOTE):** 2 credits
- May substitute with CTE courses

**Electives:** 5 credits

**Total:** 22 credits minimum

---

## Foundation + Endorsement (26 Credits Recommended)

**Endorsement Areas:**
1. STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics)
2. Business and Industry
3. Public Services
4. Arts and Humanities
5. Multidisciplinary Studies

**Requirements:**
- 4 credits in endorsement area
- Total 26 credits (22 Foundation + 4 Endorsement)

**STEM Endorsement Example:**
- Math: Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II, Pre-Calculus or higher (4 credits)
- Science: Biology, Chemistry, Physics, plus one additional (4 credits)

---

## Distinguished Level of Achievement (College-Ready)

**Requirements:**
- Foundation + Endorsement
- Algebra II (required)
- 4 credits in each of: English, Math, Science, Social Studies
- Automatic college admission to Texas public universities (top 10% class rank for UT Austin, varies for others)

---

## STAAR End-of-Course (EOC) Exams (Graduation Requirement)

**5 EOC Exams Required:**
1. English I
2. English II
3. Algebra I
4. Biology
5. US History

**Passing Requirement:**
- Approaches Grade Level or higher on each exam
- Multiple retake opportunities
- Alternate pathways if not passed (Individual Graduation Committee - IGC)

---

## State-Specific Considerations

**TEKS Standards:** All courses align to Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS)
**Dual Credit:** Texas encourages dual credit courses (high school + college credit)
**CTE:** Career and Technical Education courses can substitute for some requirements
**College Readiness:** Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board (THECB) college readiness standards

---

## College Admission (Texas Public Universities)

**Automatic Admission:**
- Top 10% class rank → UT Austin (fluctuates by year)
- Top 6-10% class rank → Other Texas public universities (varies by institution)

**Recommended Preparation:**
- Distinguished Level of Achievement
- AP/IB/Dual Credit courses
- 4 years each: English, Math (through Calculus or Pre-Calc), Science, Social Studies
- 2+ years World Language

## Quick Reference

**Every Texas HS Material Must:**
- Align to TEKS standards
- Prepare for STAAR EOC exams (English I/II, Algebra I, Biology, US History)
- Support Foundation + Endorsement pathways
- Include ELPS for English Learners
- Meet SBOE adoption criteria

**Key Point:** Texas requires STAAR EOC passage for graduation. All materials must prepare students for these high-stakes assessments while building college and career readiness.
