# MAFS Alignment Guide
**Mathematics Florida Standards for K-8 Mathematics**
**Source:** Florida Department of Education
**Applies To:** K-8 Mathematics (Florida only)

---

## Overview

**MAFS (Mathematics Florida Standards)** are Florida's mathematics content standards, based on Common Core State Standards for Mathematics (CCSS-M) with Florida-specific modifications. Florida is transitioning to **B.E.S.T. Mathematics Benchmarks** (adopted 2020), but many districts still use MAFS.

**Core Principle:** MAFS emphasizes conceptual understanding, procedural fluency, and applicationâ€”preparing students for college and career readiness.

## MAFS Standard Format

### Structure:

**Format:** `MAFS.{Grade}.{Domain}.{Cluster}.{Standard}`

**Example:** `MAFS.5.NF.1.1`

**Breakdown:**
- `MAFS` = Mathematics Florida Standards
- `5` = Grade 5
- `NF` = Number and Operationsâ€”Fractions (domain)
- `1` = Cluster 1 within NF domain
- `1` = Standard 1 within that cluster

**Full Standard Example:**
`MAFS.5.NF.1.1`: Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators (including mixed numbers) by replacing given fractions with equivalent fractions in such a way as to produce an equivalent sum or difference of fractions with like denominators.

## Standards for Mathematical Practice (SMP)

### MAFS includes 8 Standards for Mathematical Practice:

**MAFS.K12.MP.1:** Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them
**MAFS.K12.MP.2:** Reason abstractly and quantitatively
**MAFS.K12.MP.3:** Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others
**MAFS.K12.MP.4:** Model with mathematics
**MAFS.K12.MP.5:** Use appropriate tools strategically
**MAFS.K12.MP.6:** Attend to precision
**MAFS.K12.MP.7:** Look for and make use of structure
**MAFS.K12.MP.8:** Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning

**Note:** Identical to CCSS-M Mathematical Practices

**Integration:** Every lesson should address content standards AND mathematical practices

## MAFS vs. B.E.S.T. Mathematics (Transition)

### Florida is Transitioning to B.E.S.T. Mathematics:

**Adopted:** 2020
**Implementation:** Gradual rollout, varies by district
**Full Implementation:** Target 2023-2025 (varies)

**Key Differences:**

| Aspect | MAFS | B.E.S.T. Math |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **Origin** | Based on CCSS-M | Florida-developed |
| **Format** | MAFS.{Grade}.{Domain}.{Cluster}.{Std} | MA.{Grade}.{Domain}.{Standard} |
| **Practices** | 8 Math Practices (SMP) | Mathematical Thinking and Reasoning (MTRs) - 7 standards |
| **Content** | Similar to CCSS-M | Modified progressions, more explicit |
| **Example** | MAFS.5.NF.1.1 | MA.5.FR.2.1 |

**For Curriculum Development:**
- Materials created for MAFS can often be adapted to B.E.S.T. Math with standard code updates
- Content overlap is significant (~80%)
- Progressions differ in some areas

**This guide focuses on MAFS** (still widely used). B.E.S.T. Math alignment would be a separate file if needed.

## Example Alignment (Grade 5 Fractions):

**Standard:**
```
MAFS.5.NF.1.1
Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators
```

**Lesson Alignment:**
- **Introduction:** Review equivalent fractions (prerequisite)
- **Model:** Show 1/2 + 1/3 using visual models (find common denominator)
- **Guided Practice:** Students find common denominators with partner support
- **Independent Practice:** Students solve 8-10 problems with varied denominators
- **Closure:** Students explain strategy (MLR1: Stronger and Clearer Each Time)

**Mathematical Practices Addressed:**
- **MP.2:** Reason abstractly (equivalent fractions concept)
- **MP.5:** Use tools strategically (visual models, number lines)
- **MP.6:** Attend to precision (common denominators, simplifying)

**ESOL Support:**
- Visual fraction models for all students
- Sentence frames by proficiency level
- MLR8: Discussion supports for partner work
- Word wall: numerator, denominator, equivalent, common denominator

**Assessment:**
- Formative: Observation during guided practice
- Summative: 5-item quiz (mix of computation and explanation)

## Florida Math vs. California Math Comparison

### Similarities:

**Content Standards:**
- MAFS based on CCSS-M (Florida modifications minimal)
- California uses CCSS-M directly
- ~95% content overlap

**Mathematical Practices:**
- Both use 8 Standards for Mathematical Practice (identical)

**Structure:**
- Both organize by domain, cluster, standard
- Similar progression across grades

### Differences:

| Aspect | Florida (MAFS) | California (CCSS-M) |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Standard Codes** | MAFS.5.NF.1.1 | CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.5.NF.A.1 |
| **Language Support** | ESOL (WIDA) | ELD (CA-specific) |
| **Adoption** | State-level | Local districts |
| **Transition** | Moving to B.E.S.T. Math | Staying with CCSS-M |

**Practical Impact:** Materials created for MAFS work in CCSS-M states (and vice versa) with minimal modification
