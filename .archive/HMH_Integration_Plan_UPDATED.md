### 1. Texas SBOE Quality Rubric (221 lines, 6 dimensions)

**Source:** `imra25-math-k12-sboe-approved-quality-rubric.pdf`

#### **6 Quality Dimensions:**

**Implementation Quality:**
1. **Intentional Instructional Design**
   - Course-level: scope & sequence, pacing, concept connections
   - Unit-level: background knowledge, family letters (Spanish + English)
   - Lesson-level: structured plans, materials list, timing, homework guidance

2. **Progress Monitoring**
   - Variety of assessments (diagnostic, formative, summative)
   - Data analysis tools
   - Student progress tracking

3. **Supports for All Learners**
   - Differentiation for below/at/above grade level
   - Emergent Bilingual support (ELPS alignment)
   - UDL-based instructional methods

**Learning Quality:**
4. **Depth and Coherence of Key Concepts**
   - Deep understanding over lesson/unit
   - Progressive rigor increase
   - Coherence across grade levels
   - **Spaced and interleaved practice** ⚠️ NEW REQUIREMENT

5. **Balance of Conceptual and Procedural Understanding**
   - Conceptual understanding development
   - Fluency building
   - Academic mathematical language
   - Process standards integration (TEKS)
   - CRA approach (Concrete-Representational-Abstract)

6. **Productive Struggle**
   - Student self-efficacy building
   - Multiple solution paths
   - Facilitated struggle with teacher guidance

#### **Key Gaps Identified:**
- ✅ Have: Most requirements already covered
- ⚠️ **Missing:** Spaced retrieval and interleaved practice (4.3a, 4.3b)
- ⚠️ **Missing:** Progressive rigor within lessons/assessments (4.1b)
- ⚠️ **Missing:** Explicit fluency story and labeling (5.2a, 5.2c)
- ⚠️ **Missing:** Multiple solution paths emphasis (6.1b)

### 2. Webb DOK Framework (149 lines, 4 levels)

**Source:** `01_Mathematics_Revised_Webb_DOK_Definition_032016.pdf`

#### **4 DOK Levels:**

**DOK 1 (Recall)**
- Rote recall, simple procedures, well-defined algorithms
- Examples: memorized facts, basic computation, plotting points
- Key: Student either knows or doesn't - no "figuring out" required

**DOK 2 (Skill/Concept)**
- Mental processing beyond habit, decision-making
- Examples: formulating equations, organizing data, interpreting graphs
- Key: Requires conceptual understanding and explaining thinking

**DOK 3 (Strategic Thinking)**
- Reasoning with mathematical principles, non-routine problems
- Examples: novel solutions, proofs, mathematical arguments, models
- Key: Solution path not obvious, demands robust reasoning

**DOK 4 (Extended Thinking)**
- Complex work over extended time (days/weeks)
- Examples: research projects, original theorems, comprehensive models
- Key: Requires metacognition, planning, sustained work

#### **Classification Guidelines:**
- Consider complexity, not difficulty
- Consider typical student experience at grade level
- Don't rely on verbs alone - consider content complexity
- For MC items, consider whole item including distractors

**Integration Impact:**
- All assessment items must be DOK-classified
- Content Block Output List shows DOK leveling in Teacher Edition
- Requires skill: `/hmh.dok-classify`

### 4. CEID Vendor Reference Guide (1,609 lines, 11 categories)

**Source:** `CEID Vendor Reference Guide v3.pdf`

#### **11 Equity/Inclusion/Diversity Categories:**

1. **Age Equality and Physical Appearance**
   - Diverse age ranges (not just young people)
   - No stereotyping by age
   - Physical appearance diversity (body types, heights, abilities)

2. **Disability**
   - 15% representation target (matches US population)
   - Show in active, empowered roles
   - Avoid pity/charity framing
   - Use person-first OR identity-first language (context-dependent)

3. **Ethnicity, Nationality, and Race**
   - Proportional representation
   - Avoid stereotypes and tokenism
   - Specific terminology guidance (e.g., "Black" vs "African American")
   - Show diverse roles and contexts

4. **Family Structure**
   - Variety: single-parent, multi-generational, blended, chosen families
   - No assumptions about "normal" family

5. **Gender Equality and Sexual Orientation**
   - Balanced gender representation
   - Non-stereotypical roles
   - Singular "they" usage guidance
   - LGBTQ+ inclusion where age-appropriate

6. **Global and Social Issues**
   - Colonization and Indigenous Peoples (respect, accurate history)
   - Genocide (factual, age-appropriate)
   - Immigration/Refugees (humanizing, not "othering")
   - Slavery (honest history, not defining feature)

7. **Health, Safety, and Environment**
   - Nutritious foods emphasized
   - No glorification of unhealthy substances
   - Safety practices shown correctly
   - Environmental responsibility

8. **Localization**
   - International views (not US-centric)
   - Regional diversity within US
   - Animal representation (pets vs livestock contexts)

9. **Mental Health**
   - No stigmatization of mental illness
   - Trauma-informed content
   - Avoid triggering content in assessments

10. **Religion, Morality, and Politics**
    - No proselytizing
    - Factual treatment of religions
    - Avoid controversial moral positions
    - Political neutrality

11. **Socioeconomic Status**
    - Diverse contexts (not all suburban/affluent)
    - Avoid stereotypes (poverty = urban, wealth = happiness)
    - Inclusive examples

**Philosophy:**
- "Windows and Mirrors" (Rudine Sims Bishop): Content as both window (new worlds) and mirror (self-affirmation)
- Realistic AND aspirational representation
- Relevant without being controversial

**Integration Impact:**
- Requires validation skill: `/hmh.ceid-validate`
- Must check all text and images against these 11 categories
- Overlaps with Texas SBOE requirements but more comprehensive

### 5. Math Item Specifications Guide (4,956 lines, 24 question types)

**Source:** `Math Item Specs Guide (1).pdf`

#### **Critical Requirement: Digital-Print Parity**

**3 Levels of Parity:**

1. **Parity of Question Structure/Layout**
   - Visual structure must match between print and digital
   - Student should recognize same item immediately
   - ALL items can achieve this

2. **Parity of Answer Structure/Layout**
   - Interaction method should be similar
   - Answers should look the same
   - Most items can achieve this

3. **Parity of Interaction**
   - How students respond should align
   - Print writeable space = digital interaction area
   - Some limitations based on technology

**Parity Test Questions:**
1. Can student tell they're on same item as teacher (projecting digital)?
2. Can student know where to write in print when teacher inputs digital answer?
3. Can student understand print interaction given digital interaction?

**Goal Statement:**
> "Approach content and interaction choices in a way that meets the needs of the standard it's addressing and maintains the rigor of the problem in both digital & print with parity."

#### **24 Question Types (organized by category):**

**Multiple Choice:**
- Multiple Choice - Standard
- Multiple Choice - Multiple Response
- Choice Matrix - Standard

**Fill in the Blanks:**
- Cloze with Drag & Drop
- Cloze with Drop Down
- Label Image with Drag & Drop
- Label Image with Drop Down

**Classify, Match, & Order:**
- Classification
- Match List
- Order List

**Written & Recorded:**
- Essay with Rich Text

**Highlight and Drawing:**
- Drawing
- Hotspot
- Shading

**Math:**
- Math
- Label Image with Math
- Math Essay with Rich Text

**Graphing:**
- Graphing
- Number Line with Drag & Drop
- Number Line with Plot

**Charts:**
- Bar Chart
- Histogram
- Dot Plot
- Line Plot

**Additional Specifications:**
- Validation methods
- Accessibility requirements
- LaTeX code usage
- Source code editing
- Keypads for math entry

**Integration Impact:**
- Item generation must consider print AND digital simultaneously
- Requires parity validation: `/hmh.parity-validate`
- Question type selection must follow use case flow chart
