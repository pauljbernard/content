# Florida Adoption Criteria and Compliance
**Florida State Requirements for Instructional Materials**
**Source:** Florida Department of Education
**Applies To:** K-12 (Florida only, all subjects)

---

## Overview

**Florida instructional materials** must meet state-specific adoption criteria, align to B.E.S.T. Standards (ELA) and MAFS (Math), comply with content restrictions, and support ESOL students. Florida has one of the most rigorous textbook adoption processes in the nation.

**Core Principle:** Florida prioritizes content accuracy, standards alignment, accessibility, and appropriate content for all students.

---

## Florida vs. Texas vs. California

### Key Differences:

| Aspect | Florida | Texas | California |
|--------|---------|-------|------------|
| **ELA Standards** | B.E.S.T. Standards | TEKS ELA | CCSS ELA |
| **Math Standards** | MAFS (transitioning to B.E.S.T. Math) | TEKS Math | CCSS-M |
| **Language Standards** | ESOL | ELPS | ELD |
| **Content Review** | Comprehensive statute compliance | IPACC (9 categories) | Social content review |
| **Adoption Process** | State-level adoption | State-level adoption | Local adoption |

**Similarity:** All three states require explicit standards alignment, accessibility, and content appropriateness.

## B.E.S.T. Standards (Benchmarks for Excellent Student Thinking)

### ELA Standards Structure

**B.E.S.T. ELA adopted 2020, replacing Florida State Standards (FSS)**

**Format:** `ELA.{Grade}.{Strand}.{Standard}.{Sub-standard}`

**Example:** `ELA.5.R.2.1` = Grade 5, Reading, Standard 2, Sub-standard 1

**Strands:**
- **F:** Foundations (K-2 only)
- **R:** Reading
- **C:** Communication
- **V:** Vocabulary

**Emphasis:**
- Explicit instruction
- Phonics and foundational skills (K-5)
- Informational text emphasis
- Critical thinking and analysis

---

### Math Standards: MAFS â†’ B.E.S.T. Math

**MAFS (Mathematics Florida Standards):**
- Based on Common Core with Florida modifications
- Still in use in many districts (transitioning)

**B.E.S.T. Mathematics:**
- Adopted 2020, full implementation ongoing
- Less emphasis on standards for mathematical practice
- More prescriptive content progressions
- Explicit grade-level expectations

**Format:** `MA.{Grade}.{Domain}.{Standard}.{Sub-standard}`

**Example:** `MA.5.FR.2.1` = Grade 5, Fractions (FR), Standard 2, Sub-standard 1

**See Also:** `/subjects/mathematics/districts/florida/mafs-alignment.md`

## Florida Content Restrictions (F.S. 1006.31-1006.40)

### Statutory Compliance Requirements

**Similar to Texas IPACC, Florida has comprehensive content restrictions:**

---

### 1. Accuracy and Currentness (F.S. 1006.34)

**Requirement:** Materials must be factually accurate and current

**Applies To:**
- Scientific facts
- Historical information
- Geographic data
- Statistical information

**Validation:**
- Subject-matter experts review
- Sources cited and verifiable
- Up-to-date information (within 5 years for science/social studies)

---

### 2. Age-Appropriate Content (F.S. 1006.40)

**Requirement:** Materials must be age-appropriate and suitable for intended grade

**Restrictions:**
- No sexually explicit content
- No age-inappropriate violence or horror
- No promotion of illegal activities
- No profanity or obscene language

**Similar to:** Texas IPACC Category 1 (age-appropriateness)

---

### 3. Bias-Free Content (F.S. 1006.31)

**Requirement:** Free from bias, discrimination, and stereotypes

**Prohibited:**
- Racial/ethnic stereotypes
- Gender stereotypes
- Religious bias
- Socioeconomic bias
- Disability stereotypes

**Similar to:** Universal CEID guidelines, Texas IPACC Category 5

**See Also:** `/universal/content-equity/ceid-guidelines.md`

---

### 4. Instructional Standards Alignment (F.S. 1006.31)

**Requirement:** Complete alignment to B.E.S.T. Standards (ELA) or MAFS/B.E.S.T. Math

**Evidence Required:**
- Correlation charts showing coverage
- Alignment documentation
- Scope and sequence matching state standards
- Assessment aligned to standards

**Similar to:** Texas TEKS alignment requirement

---

### 5. ESOL Support (F.S. 1003.56)

**Requirement:** Materials must support English Language Learners

**Must Include:**
- Scaffolds for ESOL students
- Vocabulary support
- Multiple proficiency level accommodations
- Instructional strategies for ESOL

**Similar to:** Texas ELPS, California ELD

**See Also:** `/districts/florida/language/esol-alignment.md`

---

### 6. Accessibility (F.S. 1006.31)

**Requirement:** Materials accessible to students with disabilities

**Must Include:**
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance (digital)
- Text-to-speech compatibility
- Alt text for images
- Captions for videos
- Accessible formats available

**Similar to:** Universal accessibility requirements

**See Also:** `/universal/accessibility/wcag-compliance-guide.md`

---

### 7. Factual Holocaust Education (F.S. 1003.42)

**Requirement:** Holocaust education materials must be historically accurate and age-appropriate

**Applies to:** Social Studies, History, ELA materials including Holocaust topics

**Requirements:**
- Accurate historical information
- Primary source documents
- Age-appropriate presentation
- Consultation with Holocaust education experts

**Florida-Specific:** Unique to Florida statute

---

### 8. African American History (F.S. 1003.42)

**Requirement:** Materials must accurately portray African American history

**Applies to:** Social Studies, History, ELA

**Requirements:**
- Accurate historical representation
- Diverse perspectives included
- Contributions of African Americans highlighted
- No stereotypes or bias

**Similar to:** Universal CEID guidelines, but statutory in Florida

---

### 9. Parental Rights and Review (F.S. 1006.28)

**Requirement:** Parents have right to review and object to materials

**Process:**
- Materials available for public review
- Parents can challenge materials
- District review process required
- Transparency in adoption

**Florida Emphasis:** Strong parental rights focus

## Florida Adoption Process

### State Adoption vs. Local Adoption

**State Adoption (K-5 Reading, K-8 Math):**
- State-level review and approval
- Materials on state-adopted list
- Districts choose from approved list
- Rigorous multi-stage process

**Local Adoption (Other subjects/grades):**
- District-level review
- Must still meet statutory requirements
- Less centralized process

---

### State Adoption Timeline (Typical)

**Year 1:**
- Publishers submit materials
- Initial review for completeness

**Year 2:**
- Content expert review panels
- Statutory compliance review
- Public comment period
- State Board approval

**Year 3+:**
- Materials on approved list
- Districts adopt from list
- 5-year adoption cycle

---

## Florida Instructional Materials Review Criteria

### Evaluation Categories (similar to Texas SBOE):

**1. Content Alignment (Highest Priority)**
- 100% alignment to B.E.S.T./MAFS standards
- Depth and rigor match grade level
- Coherent scope and sequence

**2. Instructional Design**
- Research-based pedagogy
- Explicit instruction emphasis
- Scaffolded learning progressions
- Formative assessment embedded

**3. Accessibility and Equity**
- ESOL support at multiple levels
- UDL principles applied
- Accessible for students with disabilities
- Diverse representation

**4. Usability**
- Teacher-friendly
- Clear instructions
- Digital-print parity (if applicable)
- Professional development resources

**5. Assessment Quality**
- Aligned to standards
- Multiple item types
- Valid and reliable
- Actionable data for teachers

**6. Statutory Compliance**
- All F.S. 1006 requirements met
- Content restrictions followed
- Age-appropriate
- Bias-free

## Florida vs. Texas Compliance Comparison

### Similarities:

**Both Require:**
- Explicit state standards alignment (B.E.S.T./TEKS)
- Language learner support (ESOL/ELPS)
- Content restrictions (age-appropriate, bias-free)
- State-level adoption process
- Accessibility compliance
- Public review process

### Differences:

| Aspect | Florida | Texas |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Standards** | B.E.S.T. (2020) | TEKS (updated periodically) |
| **Math Standards** | MAFS/B.E.S.T. Math | TEKS Math |
| **ELL Standards** | ESOL | ELPS |
| **Adoption** | State-approved list (K-8) | State-approved list (all) |
| **Quality Rubric** | Florida IMC review | SBOE 6-dimension rubric |
| **Specific Topics** | Holocaust education, African American history (statutory) | IPACC 9 categories |

**Overall Similarity:** ~75-80% overlap in requirements

---

## Florida-California Comparison

### Similarities:

**Both:**
- Emphasize accessibility and UDL
- Require bias-free content
- Support English learners (ESOL/ELD)
- Value diverse representation

### Differences:

| Aspect | Florida | California |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **ELA Standards** | B.E.S.T. | CCSS ELA |
| **Math Standards** | MAFS/B.E.S.T. Math | CCSS-M |
| **Adoption** | State-level (K-8) | Local districts |
| **Emphasis** | Explicit instruction, phonics | Balanced literacy options |
| **Language Learners** | ESOL | ELD (3 proficiency levels) |

## Key Takeaways for Curriculum Developers

### Creating Florida-Compliant Materials:

**1. Align to B.E.S.T. Standards**
- Use exact standard codes
- Cover 100% of standards
- Match grade-level rigor

**2. Provide ESOL Support**
- Scaffolds at beginning, intermediate, advanced levels
- Vocabulary development
- Multiple access points

**3. Follow Content Restrictions**
- Age-appropriate content
- Bias-free and diverse representation
- Accurate and current information

**4. Ensure Accessibility**
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- UDL principles
- Multiple formats

**5. Emphasize Explicit Instruction**
- Florida values direct, explicit teaching
- Clear learning objectives
- Scaffolded instruction
- Formative assessment

**6. Include Assessment**
- Aligned to B.E.S.T./MAFS
- Multiple item types
- Rubrics and scoring guides
