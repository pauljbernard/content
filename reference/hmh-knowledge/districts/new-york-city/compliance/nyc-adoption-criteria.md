# New York City DOE Adoption Criteria
**District:** New York City Department of Education (NYC DOE)
**State:** New York
**Students:** 1.1M (largest US school district)
**Applies To:** All K-8 instructional materials

---

## Overview

**NYC DOE** is the largest school district in the United States, serving 1.1M students across 1,800+ schools. While NYC follows New York State standards (CCSS, NYSESLAT), the district has **additional local requirements** for curriculum adoption and implementation.

**Key Principle:** Materials must meet both NYS state standards AND NYC DOE district-specific criteria.

---

## Relationship to New York State

### State Standards (Base Requirements):

NYC DOE follows all New York State standards:
- **Content Standards:** CCSS (Math and ELA), NGSS (Science)
- **Language Standards:** NYSESLAT (NYS English as a Second Language Achievement Test)
- **State Assessments:** NYS Grades 3-8 Testing Program

**See:** `/districts/new-york/compliance/new-york-adoption-criteria.md` for state-level requirements

---

### NYC District Layer (Additional Requirements):

NYC DOE adds district-specific requirements on top of state standards:

1. **Passport to Social Studies** (Social Studies framework)
2. **NYC Science Scope & Sequence** (NGSS implementation)
3. **Culturally Responsive-Sustaining Education** (CR-SE Framework)
4. **Universal Literacy** (district literacy initiative)
5. **NYC Digital Equity** (technology access requirements)
6. **Multilingual Learner Success** (MLL supports beyond NYSESLAT)

---

## NYC DOE Adoption Process

### 1. Initial Review (NYC DOE Staff)

**Materials submitted must include:**
- Alignment to NYS standards (CCSS, NGSS, etc.)
- Evidence of NYC-specific requirements (CR-SE, Universal Literacy, etc.)
- Scope and sequence documents
- Sample lessons and assessments
- Digital/print formats
- Professional learning plans

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks for initial review

---

### 2. Pilot Programs (Schools-Based)

**NYC typically requires:**
- Pilot implementation in 10-20 schools
- Diverse school representation (demographics, performance levels, boroughs)
- Teacher and student feedback collection
- Usage data and analytics
- Achievement data (if available)

**Timeline:** 1-2 semesters (6-12 months)

---

### 3. Full Adoption Review

**Decision Factors:**
- Pilot program results
- Alignment to NYC priorities (equity, multilingual learners, literacy)
- Cost and scalability
- Professional development quality
- Digital platform quality
- Parent/community feedback

**Timeline:** 2-3 months for final decision

---

### 4. Implementation Support

**If approved, publishers must provide:**
- District-wide professional development
- Implementation guides for schools
- Technical support for digital platforms
- Ongoing content updates
- Data reporting and analytics

---

## NYC-Specific Requirements

### 1. Culturally Responsive-Sustaining Education (CR-SE)

**NYC DOE Framework:** All materials must demonstrate CR-SE principles

**CR-SE Components:**

**Welcoming and Affirming Environment:**
- Diverse representation in texts, images, examples
- Multiple cultural perspectives included
- Students see themselves reflected in materials
- Inclusive language throughout

**High Expectations and Rigorous Instruction:**
- All students can achieve at high levels
- Scaffolds provided without lowering expectations
- Growth mindset language
- Challenge for all learners

**Inclusive Curriculum and Assessment:**
- Multiple ways to demonstrate learning
- Culturally relevant contexts and examples
- Assessments free from cultural bias
- Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

**Ongoing Professional Learning:**
- Teacher materials include CR-SE guidance
- Professional development on equity and inclusion
- Resources for culturally responsive teaching

**See:** NYC DOE CR-SE Framework (https://www.schools.nyc.gov/about-us/vision-and-mission/culturally-responsive-sustaining-education)

---

### 2. Passport to Social Studies (NYC Framework)

**NYC-specific social studies framework** (builds on NYS standards)

**Components:**
- Inquiry-based learning (C3 Framework aligned)
- NYC history and civics emphasis
- Document-based questions (DBQs)
- Current events integration
- Civic action projects

**Grade-Level Focus:**
- K-2: My Community (NYC neighborhoods)
- Grade 3: NYC Geography and Communities
- Grade 4: NYC and NYS History
- Grade 5: US History (with NYC connections)
- Grade 6-8: Global history (with NYC immigration stories)

**Required Elements:**
- Primary source analysis
- Multiple perspectives on historical events
- NYC-specific case studies
- Civic engagement opportunities

---

### 3. Universal Literacy Initiative

**NYC DOE literacy framework** (across all subjects)

**Components:**

**Reading:**
- Complex text exposure (grade-level and above)
- Close reading protocols
- Annotation strategies
- Text-dependent questions
- Multiple text types (literary, informational, multimedia)

**Writing:**
- Writing across all subjects
- Evidence-based arguments
- Research and inquiry
- Peer review and revision
- Publishing and presentation

**Speaking and Listening:**
- Academic discussions
- Collaborative learning
- Presentation skills
- Active listening

**Language:**
- Vocabulary development
- Grammar in context
- Domain-specific language
- Language for academic success

**Cross-Subject Application:**
All materials (Math, Science, Social Studies) must include literacy components

---

### 4. Multilingual Learner (MLL) Success

**NYC serves 150,000+ Multilingual Learners** (formerly ELLs)

**Beyond NYSESLAT (state requirement), NYC requires:**

**Language Supports:**
- Scaffolds at 3 proficiency levels (Entering, Emerging, Transitioning, Expanding, Commanding)
- Sentence frames and stems
- Visual supports and graphic organizers
- Bilingual glossaries (top languages: Spanish, Chinese, Arabic, Bengali)
- Translation supports for families

**Instructional Strategies:**
- Integrated content and language instruction
- Collaborative learning opportunities
- Academic conversation protocols
- Vocabulary pre-teaching and reinforcement
- Home language as asset (not deficit)

**Assessment:**
- Accommodations for MLLs
- Language-free assessment options where appropriate
- Progress monitoring in English development
- Multiple ways to demonstrate understanding

**Family Engagement:**
- Materials available in multiple languages
- Family guides in home languages
- Communication tools for non-English speaking families

---

### 5. NYC Digital Equity

**All students must have access to digital materials**

**Requirements:**

**Device Access:**
- Materials must work on NYC-issued devices (iPads, Chromebooks, laptops)
- Offline access options for students without internet at home
- Mobile-responsive design

**Accessibility:**
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance (minimum)
- Screen reader compatible
- Keyboard navigation
- Adjustable text size and contrast
- Closed captions on all videos

**Data Privacy:**
- FERPA compliance
- NYC DOE data privacy requirements
- No sale of student data
- Transparent data usage policies
- Parent opt-out options

**Technical Support:**
- Help desk for families and teachers
- Multilingual support
- Clear troubleshooting guides
- Regular updates and bug fixes

---

### 6. Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)

**NYC DOE promotes SEL across all subjects**

**SEL Competencies (integrated into materials):**

**Self-Awareness:**
- Reflection opportunities
- Growth mindset language
- Identity exploration

**Self-Management:**
- Goal-setting activities
- Organization strategies
- Stress management techniques

**Social Awareness:**
- Empathy development
- Perspective-taking
- Respect for diversity

**Relationship Skills:**
- Collaborative learning
- Communication skills
- Conflict resolution

**Responsible Decision-Making:**
- Problem-solving
- Ethical reasoning
- Civic responsibility

## NYC Assessment and Accountability

### State Assessments:

**NYC students take NYS assessments:**
- Grades 3-8 ELA and Math (annual)
- Grade 4 and 8 Science (NGSS-aligned)
- NYSESLAT (for MLLs)

**Materials must prepare students for these assessments**

---

### NYC Benchmark Assessments:

**District uses benchmark/interim assessments:**
- Periodic Assessments (fall, winter, spring)
- Formative assessment tools
- Progress monitoring systems
- Data dashboards for teachers and leaders

**Materials should align with or supplement these assessments**

---

### School Quality Reports:

**NYC rates schools on multiple factors:**
- Student achievement (test scores)
- Student progress (growth)
- School environment (surveys)
- College/career readiness

**Materials should support school improvement in these areas**

- [ ] MLL supports at 5 proficiency levels
- [ ] Digital equity requirements met
- [ ] SEL competencies integrated

- [ ] Items aligned to NYS assessment format
- [ ] Rigor matches NYS tests (DOK levels)
- [ ] Formative assessment tools included
- [ ] Data reporting compatible with NYC systems

### Equity and Access:
- [ ] Diverse representation in content
- [ ] Culturally relevant examples and contexts
- [ ] UDL principles applied
- [ ] Accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA)
- [ ] Family materials in multiple languages
