# Historical Thinking Practices
**Source:** Based on Historical Thinking Project, C3 Framework
**Applies To:** K-12 Social Studies (all states)

---

## Overview

**Historical thinking practices** help students analyze the past, understand causation, evaluate sources, and develop evidence-based arguments.

---

## Key Historical Thinking Concepts

**1. Chronological Thinking**
- Sequence events, distinguish past/present/future
- Identify patterns of continuity and change

**2. Historical Comprehension**
- Reconstruct the literal meaning of historical passages
- Identify central questions, read historical narratives

**3. Historical Analysis and Interpretation**
- Compare/contrast different sets of ideas and values
- Consider multiple perspectives
- Analyze cause-and-effect relationships

**4. Historical Research**
- Formulate historical questions from encounters with sources
- Obtain and interrogate historical data from various sources
- Support interpretations with historical evidence

**5. Historical Issue-Analysis and Decision-Making**
- Identify problems and dilemmas in the past
- Analyze interests and values of people involved
- Evaluate alternative courses of action
- Prepare and defend positions based on evidence

---

## Application in Lessons

**Scaffolds for Different Grade Levels:**
- **K-2:** Simple timelines, sequencing, "then and now"
- **3-5:** Multiple perspectives, cause/effect, primary vs. secondary sources
- **6-8:** Complex causation, bias analysis, corroborating sources
- **9-12:** Historiography, contextual analysis, synthesis of multiple sources
