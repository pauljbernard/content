# IB Learner Profile
**Applies To:** All IB programmes (PYP, MYP, DP, CP)
**Level:** Ages 3-19

---

## Overview

The IB Learner Profile describes 10 attributes that IB programmes aim to develop in students. These attributes represent the ideal qualities of lifelong learners.

**Key Principle:** "The IB Learner Profile is the guiding framework for all IB teaching and learning."

---

## The 10 Attributes

### 1. Inquirers
We nurture curiosity, developing skills for inquiry and research. We know how to learn independently and with others. We learn with enthusiasm and sustain our love of learning throughout life.

**In Practice:**
- Ask meaningful questions
- Conduct investigations
- Research independently
- Demonstrate curiosity

### 2. Knowledgeable
We develop and use conceptual understanding, exploring knowledge across disciplines. We engage with issues and ideas that have local and global significance.

**In Practice:**
- Deep conceptual understanding
- Interdisciplinary connections
- Local and global perspectives

### 3. Thinkers
We use critical and creative thinking skills to analyze complex problems and take responsible action. We exercise initiative in making reasoned, ethical decisions.

**In Practice:**
- Critical thinking
- Creative problem-solving
- Ethical decision-making

### 4. Communicators
We express ourselves confidently and creatively in more than one language and in many ways. We collaborate effectively, listening carefully to others' perspectives.

**In Practice:**
- Multilingual communication
- Collaborative skills
- Active listening
- Multiple forms of expression

### 5. Principled
We act with integrity and honesty, with a strong sense of fairness and justice, and with respect for the dignity and rights of people everywhere. We take responsibility for our actions and consequences.

**In Practice:**
- Ethical behavior
- Honesty and integrity
- Sense of justice
- Personal responsibility

### 6. Open-Minded
We critically appreciate our own cultures and personal histories, as well as the values and traditions of others. We seek and evaluate a range of points of view, and are willing to grow from the experience.

**In Practice:**
- Cultural awareness
- Multiple perspectives
- Reflection and growth
- Challenging own assumptions

### 7. Caring
We show empathy, compassion and respect. We have a commitment to service, and we act to make a positive difference in others' lives and in the world around us.

**In Practice:**
- Empathy and compassion
- Service and action
- Environmental stewardship
- Making a positive difference

### 8. Risk-Takers
We approach uncertainty with forethought and determination. We work independently and cooperatively to explore new ideas and innovative strategies. We are resourceful and resilient in the face of challenges.

**In Practice:**
- Courage to try new things
- Resilience
- Innovation
- Embracing challenges

### 9. Balanced
We understand the importance of balancing different aspects of our lives—intellectual, physical, and emotional—to achieve well-being for ourselves and others. We recognize our interdependence with other people and with the world we share.

**In Practice:**
- Physical, emotional, intellectual balance
- Well-being
- Interdependence awareness

### 10. Reflective
We thoughtfully consider the world and our own ideas and experience. We work to understand our strengths and weaknesses to support our learning and personal development.

**In Practice:**
- Self-reflection
- Metacognition
- Learning from experience
- Personal growth

## Integration Across IB Programmes

**PYP (Ages 3-12):**
- Learner profile introduced through age-appropriate language
- Embedded in units of inquiry
- Students reflect on which attributes they demonstrate

**MYP (Ages 11-16):**
- Learner profile explicitly taught and assessed
- Rubrics incorporate learner profile attributes
- Service as Action develops caring and principled attributes

**DP (Ages 16-19):**
- Learner profile central to CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service)
- Theory of Knowledge develops thinkers and reflective students
- Extended Essay requires inquirers

---

## Quick Reference

**Every IB Lesson Should:**
- Connect to one or more learner profile attributes
- Make attributes explicit to students
- Provide opportunities to develop and demonstrate attributes
- Include reflection on learner profile growth
