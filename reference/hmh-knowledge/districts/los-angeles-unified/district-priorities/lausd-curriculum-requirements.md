# LAUSD Curriculum Requirements and Priorities  
**District:** Los Angeles Unified School District
**Applies To:** All K-8 instructional materials

---

## Overview

LAUSD has several district-wide priorities beyond California state standards that apply to all curriculum materials.

---

## 1. Graduation for All

**Goal:** 100% graduation, college and career ready

**K-8 Implications:**
- Build foundational skills for secondary success
- Growth mindset and persistence
- Critical thinking and problem-solving
- 21st-century skills (4 Cs: Collaboration, Communication, Creativity, Critical Thinking)
- Age-appropriate career exploration

**Materials should:**
- Connect to real-world applications
- Develop academic language
- Foster independence and self-directed learning
- Prepare for A-G requirements (CA college prep)

---

## 2. Arts Integration

**LAUSD Arts Education Branch** promotes arts across all subjects

**Integration Examples:**
- **Math:** Geometric art, music/rhythm and fractions, data visualization
- **Science:** Scientific illustration, model-making, photography
- **ELA:** Reader's theater, visual storytelling, poetry performance
- **Social Studies:** Historical art analysis, artifact creation

**Materials should include:**
- Arts-integrated projects and activities
- Creative expression options
- Visual, musical, dramatic modalities
- Connections to CA Arts Standards

---

## 3. Social Justice Standards

**LAUSD adopts Teaching Tolerance Social Justice Standards**

**4 Domains:**
1. **Identity** - Positive social identities
2. **Diversity** - Comfort with people different from self
3. **Justice** - Recognize unfairness, develop tools to address
4. **Action** - Plan and carry out collective action

**In Materials:**
- Diverse representation (race, ethnicity, language, ability, family structure)
- Multiple perspectives on historical/current events
- Critical analysis of fairness and equity
- Opportunities for civic engagement and action

---

## 4. LAUSD Local Assessments

**District assessment system:**
- Common assessments (district-created, aligned to CCSS)
- Interim/benchmark assessments (3-4 times per year)
- Performance tasks
- Formative assessment embedded

**Materials should:**
- Align to CAASPP (Smarter Balanced) format
- Include embedded formative assessments
- Provide data dashboards for teachers
- Support intervention and differentiation based on data
