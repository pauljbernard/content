# North Carolina Adoption Criteria
**State Board of Education Requirements**
**Source:** North Carolina Department of Public Instruction (NCDPI)
**Applies To:** All North Carolina instructional materials (all subjects)

---

## Overview

**North Carolina has a formal statewide textbook adoption process** through the State Board of Education. All materials must align to NC Standard Course of Study and support English Learners.

---

## Key Facts

| Aspect | North Carolina |
|--------|----------------|
| **Content Standards** | NC Standard Course of Study - CCSS-based |
| **Language Standards** | English Learners (WIDA consortium) |
| **Adoption Process** | State adoption + local selection |
| **Compliance** | NCDPI review process |
| **Science Standards** | NC Science Standards (NGSS-based) |
| **Assessment** | NC EOG (3-8), NC EOC (HS) |
| **EL Assessment** | ACCESS for ELLs (WIDA) |

---

## NC Standard Course of Study

**Mathematics:** NC Math Standards (based on CCSS-M with modifications)

**ELA:** NC ELA Standards (based on CCSS-ELA)

**Science:** NC Science Standards (based on NGSS)

**Note:** NC has more substantial modifications to CCSS than most states, but still 85-90% aligned.

---

## State Adoption Process

**Unlike most CCSS states, NC has formal state adoption:**
- Materials reviewed by NCDPI committees
- Approved materials placed on state adoption list
- Districts select from approved list
- Multi-year adoption cycles by subject

**Similar to Texas and California models**

---

## English Learner Support

**North Carolina is a WIDA consortium member:**
- WIDA ELD Standards
- ACCESS for ELLs
- 6 proficiency levels

**See:** `/districts/illinois/language/illinois-el-standards.md` for complete WIDA guidance

---

## Knowledge Reuse (90%+)

**Reused:** CCSS-M (85-90% aligned), NGSS, WIDA, MLRs, universal frameworks

**NC-Specific:** Some unique NC standards, state adoption process
