# Miami-Dade ELL Framework
**District:** Miami-Dade County Public Schools
**ELLs:** 70,000+ (21% of enrollment)
**Languages:** 90+, predominantly Spanish (90%+ of ELLs), then Haitian Creole, Portuguese, French
**Assessment:** WIDA ACCESS for ELLs 2.0

---

## Overview

M-DCPS operates the **largest bilingual education program in the United States**, serving 70,000+ ELLs. Over 90% speak Spanish at home, with significant Haitian Creole, Portuguese, and French populations.

**See:** `/districts/florida/language/florida-el-standards.md` for Florida ESOL/WIDA framework (90% reuse)

---

## WIDA 6 Proficiency Levels

M-DCPS uses WIDA (same as Florida state):
1. Entering
2. Emerging
3. Developing
4. Expanding
5. Bridging
6. Reaching

---

## M-DCPS Bilingual Programs

### 1. Bilingual Education (Spanish/English):
- Instruction in both languages
- Goal: Bilingualism and biliteracy
- Majority of ELL students
- Strong community support

### 2. ESOL (English for Speakers of Other Languages):
- English-focused instruction with ESOL strategies
- All content areas
- Sheltered instruction

### 3. Heritage Language Programs:
- Spanish for heritage speakers
- Maintain/develop home language literacy
- Cultural connection

### 4. Dual Language Immersion:
- 50/50 or 90/10 models
- ELLs and native English speakers together
- Growing program (30+ schools)

---

## Critical Language Supports

**Spanish (90%+ of ELLs):**
- Bilingual glossaries (Spanish/English) ESSENTIAL
- Spanish cognates highlighted
- Spanish-language materials available
- Cultural references to Latin America, Caribbean, Spain

**Haitian Creole (second largest):**
- Haitian Creole glossaries for key content
- Cultural responsiveness to Haitian community
- Translation support for families
