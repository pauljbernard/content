# College and Career Readiness Framework (Grades 9-12)
**Applies To:** All high school instructional materials (grades 9-12)
**Purpose:** Ensure all students graduate ready for college and career success

---

## Overview

High school education must prepare ALL students for postsecondary success, whether college, career training, military, or workforce entry. Materials must build college-level literacy, numeracy, critical thinking, and career skills.

## College Readiness Standards

### Academic Preparation

**English/Language Arts:**
- Read and comprehend complex texts (college-level)
- Write evidence-based arguments and analyses
- Conduct research with credible sources
- Present ideas clearly and persuasively

**Mathematics:**
- Algebra I, Algebra II, Geometry proficiency (minimum)
- Pre-Calculus and Calculus (college-bound)
- Statistics and data analysis
- Mathematical reasoning and problem-solving

**Science:**
- Biology, Chemistry, Physics foundation
- Scientific inquiry and investigation
- Data analysis and interpretation
- STEM pathways (for STEM-bound students)

**Social Studies:**
- US History, World History, Government, Economics
- Historical thinking and analysis
- Civic knowledge and engagement
- Global awareness

---

### College Entrance Requirements

**Common Requirements (varies by state/college):**
- 4 years English
- 3-4 years Math (through Algebra II minimum)
- 2-3 years Science (including lab sciences)
- 2-3 years Social Studies
- 2+ years World Language (many colleges)
- Fine Arts, PE, electives

**State-Specific "A-G" or College Prep Requirements:**
- **California:** A-G requirements for UC/CSU eligibility
- **Texas:** Foundation High School Program + Endorsements
- **Florida:** 24-credit standard diploma
- **Others:** See state-specific graduation requirements

## Advanced Coursework Options

### Advanced Placement (AP)

**College Board AP Program:**
- College-level courses in high school
- AP Exams (scored 1-5, 3+ often earns college credit)
- 38 AP courses across 7 subject areas
- Demonstrates rigor for college admissions

**Common AP Courses:**
- **Math:** AP Calculus AB/BC, AP Statistics
- **English:** AP English Language, AP English Literature
- **Science:** AP Biology, AP Chemistry, AP Physics, AP Environmental Science
- **Social Studies:** AP US History, AP World History, AP Government, AP Economics
- **Computer Science:** AP Computer Science A, AP Computer Science Principles
- **World Languages:** AP Spanish, AP French, AP Chinese, etc.

**Materials Must:**
- Align to AP Course and Exam Descriptions
- Prepare students for AP Exam format and rigor
- Include practice exams and released questions
- Support AP instructional strategies

---

### International Baccalaureate (IB)

**IB Diploma Programme (grades 11-12):**
- Internationally recognized curriculum
- 6 subject groups + Core (TOK, EE, CAS)
- Assessed internally and externally
- Highly rigorous, holistic education

**IB Middle Years Programme (MYP, grades 6-10):**
- Prepares for DP
- Interdisciplinary learning
- Community service

**Materials Should Support:**
- IB approaches to learning (ATL)
- Inquiry-based instruction
- International-mindedness
- Conceptual understanding

---

### Dual Enrollment / Early College

**Concurrent Enrollment:**
- High school students take college courses
- Earn HS + college credit simultaneously
- Often at community colleges or universities
- Accelerates degree completion

**Early College High Schools:**
- Integrated HS + college
- Graduate with HS diploma + Associate's degree (or credits)
- Often partnership with community college

**Materials Should:**
- Meet college-level rigor
- Align to postsecondary standards
- Prepare for college expectations

## Graduation Requirements

### State Graduation Requirements (Examples)

**Texas:**
- Foundation High School Program (22 credits minimum)
- Endorsements: STEM, Business/Industry, Public Service, Arts/Humanities, Multidisciplinary
- Distinguished Level of Achievement (for top students)

**California:**
- 230 credits minimum
- A-G requirements for UC/CSU eligibility
- College/Career Indicator (state accountability)

**Florida:**
- 24 credits (standard diploma)
- Algebra I, Geometry, Biology I EOCs
- Civics Literacy, Financial Literacy requirements

**See state-specific files for complete requirements**

---

### Assessment Requirements

**State End-of-Course (EOC) Exams:**
- Required for graduation in many states
- Common subjects: Algebra I, English I/II, Biology, US History

**State Exit Exams:**
- Some states require passing comprehensive exam
- Measures readiness for postsecondary

**College Entrance Exams:**
- SAT, ACT (optional for most colleges post-2020, but still valuable)
- Materials should develop skills tested (reading comprehension, math reasoning, data analysis, writing)

## Integration Across All HS Materials

### In Every Subject:

**1. College-Level Literacy:**
- Complex text reading (DOK 3-4)
- Academic writing with evidence
- Research and citation skills
- Disciplinary vocabulary

**2. Career Connections:**
- Real-world applications
- Industry examples and case studies
- Guest speakers / field trips (suggested)
- Work-based learning opportunities

**3. Critical Thinking:**
- Analysis and evaluation
- Problem-solving with multiple solutions
- Decision-making with trade-offs
- Argument construction and critique

**4. College/Career Exploration:**
- Exposure to college majors and careers
- Skills inventories and interest surveys
- Postsecondary planning resources
- Financial aid awareness

## Quick Reference

**All HS Materials Must:**
- Build college-level literacy and numeracy
- Connect to careers and real-world applications
- Develop critical thinking and problem-solving
- Support advanced coursework options (AP, IB, dual enrollment)
- Align to state graduation requirements
- Prepare for college entrance and career entry
- Include employability skills (4 Cs)

**Remember:** High school is THE gateway to postsecondary success. Every course, every lesson must ask: "Does this prepare students for college AND career?"
