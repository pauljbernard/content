# Los Angeles Unified School District Adoption Criteria
**District:** Los Angeles Unified School District (LAUSD)
**State:** California
**Students:** 600,000+ (second largest US school district)
**Applies To:** All K-8 instructional materials

---

## Overview

**LAUSD** is the second largest school district in the United States, serving over 600,000 students across Los Angeles County. While LAUSD follows California state standards (CCSS, NGSS, CA ELD), the district has **additional local requirements** for curriculum adoption and implementation.

**Key Principle:** Materials must meet both California state standards AND LAUSD district-specific criteria.

---

## Relationship to California State

### State Standards (Base Requirements):

LAUSD follows all California state standards:
- **Content Standards:** CCSS (Math and ELA), NGSS (Science), CA History-Social Science Framework
- **Language Standards:** CA ELD (English Language Development) Standards
- **State Assessments:** CAASPP (Smarter Balanced for ELA/Math, CAST for Science)

**See:** `/districts/california/compliance/california-adoption-criteria.md` for state-level requirements

---

### LAUSD District Layer (Additional Requirements):

LAUSD adds district-specific requirements on top of state standards:

1. **100,000 English Learners Served:** Robust EL supports beyond state requirements
2. **Multilingual and Multicultural Education Policy:** Cultural responsiveness and multilingualism
3. **Graduation for All:** College and career readiness focus (even in elementary)
4. **Arts Integration:** Arts across all subjects
5. **Social Justice Standards:** Equity and anti-bias education
6. **Digital Equity:** Technology access for all students

---

## LAUSD Adoption Process

### 1. Needs Assessment (District-Led)

**LAUSD identifies curriculum needs:**
- Subject area priority
- Alignment with district goals
- Budget considerations
- Input from teachers, principals, community

**Timeline:** Ongoing, determines adoption calendar

---

### 2. Piloting Phase (School-Based)

**Pilot program requirements:**
- Materials piloted in 15-30 schools
- Diverse school representation (demographics, performance, regions)
- Teacher surveys and feedback
- Student outcome data collected
- Usage and engagement metrics

**Timeline:** Full academic year (9-10 months)

---

### 3. Review and Selection (Committee)

**LAUSD Instructional Materials Committee:**
- Teachers, administrators, specialists, community members
- Review pilot data and feedback
- Evaluate alignment to state and district standards
- Assess equity and cultural responsiveness
- Cost-benefit analysis

**Timeline:** 2-4 months after pilot

---

### 4. Board Approval (LAUSD Board of Education)

**Final approval by school board:**
- Public presentation
- Community input session
- Board vote
- Contract negotiation

**Timeline:** 1-2 months

---

### 5. Implementation Support

**If approved, publishers must provide:**
- District-wide professional development (PD for 10,000+ teachers)
- Implementation timelines and guides
- Technical support for digital platforms
- Ongoing PD and coaching
- Data and analytics tools
- Assessment integration

---

## LAUSD-Specific Requirements

### 1. English Learner Support (100,000+ ELs)

**LAUSD serves 100,000+ English Learners** (~17% of enrollment)

**Top Languages:**
- Spanish (majority)
- Armenian
- Korean
- Filipino/Tagalog
- Cantonese/Mandarin
- Arabic

---

#### Designated and Integrated ELD:

**LAUSD requires both:**

**Designated ELD:**
- Dedicated time for English language development
- Explicit language instruction
- Focus on CA ELD standards
- Stand-alone or within ELA block

**Integrated ELD:**
- Language development across all subjects
- Content instruction with ELD scaffolds
- Academic language in Math, Science, Social Studies
- All teachers are language teachers

---

#### ELD Scaffolds Beyond CA Standards:

**LAUSD expects robust scaffolds:**

**Linguistic Supports:**
- Sentence frames for all 5 CA ELD proficiency levels
- Cognates (especially Spanish-English)
- Bilingual glossaries (top 6 languages)
- Academic language development routines

**Instructional Strategies:**
- SDAIE (Specially Designed Academic Instruction in English)
- Collaborative learning structures
- Visual and graphic supports
- Home language support (where feasible)
- Extended time and flexible pacing

**Assessment:**
- ELPAC (state assessment) preparation
- Formative language assessment
- Multiple ways to demonstrate understanding
- Accommodations for ELs

**Family Engagement:**
- Translated materials (top languages)
- Family guides in home languages
- Multilingual communication

**See:** `/districts/los-angeles-unified/language/lausd-el-framework.md` for detailed EL supports

---

### 2. Multilingual and Multicultural Education (MME) Policy

**LAUSD Board Policy:** All students receive multilingual and multicultural education

---

#### Multilingual Components:

**Language as Asset:**
- Home languages valued and leveraged
- Translanguaging encouraged
- Dual Language programs supported
- World Language opportunities

**Examples:**
- Materials include Spanish/English cognates
- Home language connections in activities
- Bilingual resources provided
- Language diversity celebrated

---

#### Multicultural Components:

**Cultural Responsiveness:**
- Diverse representation in texts, images, examples
- Multiple cultural perspectives
- Students see themselves in materials
- Culturally relevant contexts and problems

**LAUSD Serves:**
- 90+ languages spoken
- Diverse racial/ethnic communities (Hispanic/Latino 73%, African American 8%, White 10%, Asian 5%, other 4%)
- Wide range of immigration experiences
- Varied socioeconomic backgrounds

**Examples:**
- Word problems featuring diverse names and contexts
- Historical events from multiple perspectives
- Literature from diverse authors and cultures
- Visual representation of diversity

---

#### Social Justice Standards Integration:

**LAUSD adopts Teaching Tolerance Social Justice Standards:**

**4 Domains:**
1. **Identity:** Students will develop positive social identities
2. **Diversity:** Students will express comfort with people different from themselves
3. **Justice:** Students will recognize unfairness and will develop tools to address it
4. **Action:** Students will plan and carry out collective action

**Integration in Materials:**
- Diverse representation (identity)
- Multiple perspectives (diversity)
- Critical thinking about fairness (justice)
- Civic engagement opportunities (action)

**See:** Teaching Tolerance Social Justice Standards (https://www.learningforjustice.org/frameworks/social-justice-standards)

---

### 3. Graduation for All Initiative

**Goal:** 100% of students graduate ready for college and career

**Even in Elementary (K-5), materials should:**
- Build foundational skills for secondary success
- Develop critical thinking and problem-solving
- Foster growth mindset
- Connect to college and career themes (age-appropriate)

**Middle School (6-8), materials must:**
- Prepare for rigorous high school coursework (A-G requirements)
- Include career exploration
- Develop 21st-century skills (collaboration, communication, creativity, critical thinking)
- Build academic language and literacy

---

### 4. Arts Integration

**LAUSD Arts Education** branch requires arts integration across subjects

**Arts Integration Principles:**
- Arts not just as separate subject, but woven into Math, ELA, Science, Social Studies
- Creative expression and exploration
- Multiple modalities for learning

**Examples:**
- Math: Geometric art, music and fractions, data visualization as art
- Science: Scientific drawing, model-making, nature photography
- ELA: Reader's theater, visual storytelling, poetry and song
- Social Studies: Historical art analysis, create historical artifacts, documentary filmmaking

**Materials Should Include:**
- Arts-integrated activities and projects
- Creative expression options
- Visual, musical, dramatic, and kinesthetic learning opportunities

---

### 5. Digital Equity and Access

**LAUSD commitment:** All students have access to digital learning

**Requirements:**

**Device Compatibility:**
- Materials work on Chromebooks (LAUSD standard device)
- Mobile-responsive for smartphone access (many families)
- Offline options for students without home internet
- Low-bandwidth versions

**Accessibility:**
- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Screen reader compatible
- Keyboard navigation
- Adjustable settings (text size, contrast, audio)
- Closed captions on all videos

**Technical Support:**
- Multilingual help desk
- Troubleshooting guides (text and video)
- Teacher tech support
- Regular updates

**Data Privacy:**
- Student Data Privacy Agreement (LAUSD requirement)
- FERPA and COPPA compliance
- No sale of student data
- Transparent privacy policies

## LAUSD Assessment and Accountability

### State Assessments (CAASPP):

**LAUSD students take CA state tests:**
- Grades 3-8 ELA and Math (Smarter Balanced)
- Grades 5 and 8 Science (CAST)
- ELPAC (for English Learners)

**Materials must align to CAASPP format and rigor**

---

### LAUSD Local Assessments:

**District uses local assessments:**
- Common assessments (district-created)
- Performance tasks
- Interim/benchmark assessments (3-4 times per year)
- Formative assessment tools

**Materials should align with or supplement LAUSD assessment system**

---

### School Performance Framework:

**LAUSD measures schools on:**
- Student achievement (test scores)
- Student progress (growth)
- College/career readiness indicators
- Climate and culture (surveys)

**Materials should support improvement in these areas**

---

## Content Alignment Checklist

### California State Standards Alignment:
- [ ] CCSS Math alignment (grades K-8)
- [ ] CCSS ELA alignment (grades K-8)
- [ ] NGSS alignment (where applicable)
- [ ] CA History-Social Science Framework alignment
- [ ] CA ELD Standards alignment (5 proficiency levels)

### LAUSD District Requirements:
- [ ] Robust EL supports (Designated and Integrated ELD)
- [ ] Multilingual education components (home language as asset)
- [ ] Multicultural education (diverse representation, multiple perspectives)
- [ ] Social Justice Standards integration
- [ ] College/career readiness connections (even in elementary)
- [ ] Arts integration opportunities
- [ ] Digital equity (Chromebook compatible, offline options, accessibility)

### Assessment Alignment:
- [ ] CAASPP/Smarter Balanced format and rigor
- [ ] Items aligned to CA standards
- [ ] Performance tasks included
- [ ] Formative assessment tools
- [ ] Data reporting features

### Equity and Access:
- [ ] Culturally responsive and sustaining
- [ ] Linguistic accessibility (scaffolds for ELs)
- [ ] WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility
- [ ] Supports for diverse learners (UDL)
- [ ] Family materials in multiple languages

- Review: 2-4 months
- Board Approval: 1-2 months
