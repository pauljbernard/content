# High School Assessment Guide (Grades 9-12)
**Applies To:** All high school assessments

---

## Overview

HS assessments must measure college/career readiness, align to state standards and graduation requirements, and prepare students for postsecondary assessments.

---

## Assessment Types

### 1. Formative Assessments
- Exit tickets, quick checks
- Discussion participation
- Draft reviews
- Self-assessment and reflection

### 2. Summative Assessments
- Unit tests and exams
- End-of-Course (EOC) exams (state-required)
- Final exams (semester/year-end)
- Performance tasks and projects

### 3. Performance-Based Assessments
- Research papers
- Presentations and defenses
- Labs and investigations (Science)
- Portfolios
- Exhibitions

### 4. College Entrance Test Preparation
- Practice SAT/ACT questions
- AP Exam practice
- Released test items

---

## State-Required Assessments

**End-of-Course (EOC) Exams (varies by state):**
- Common subjects: Algebra I, English I/II, Biology, US History
- Often required for graduation
- May be used for state accountability

**Graduation Tests:**
- Some states require passing comprehensive exam

**See state-specific files for requirements**

---

## Rigor and Complexity

**DOK Levels (emphasize 3-4 in HS):**
- DOK 3: Strategic Thinking (most HS assessments)
- DOK 4: Extended Thinking (research papers, projects)

**Bloom's Taxonomy (upper levels):**
- Analyzing
- Evaluating
- Creating
