# High School Chemistry Guide
**Course Level:** Typically Grade 10-11 (Chemistry), Grade 11-12 (AP Chemistry)
**Prerequisites:** Algebra I (for regular Chem); Chemistry + Algebra II (for AP Chem)
**Applies To:** All high school Chemistry and AP Chemistry courses

---

## Overview

Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, composition, structure, and the changes it undergoes. High school chemistry introduces atomic structure, bonding, reactions, stoichiometry, and thermodynamics. AP Chemistry is a college-level course emphasizing quantitative problem-solving, lab investigations, and molecular-level understanding.

**College/Career Readiness:** Chemistry is required for STEM majors (medicine, engineering, pharmacy, chemistry, materials science) and essential for understanding materials, energy, and environmental issues.

**State Requirements:** Chemistry (with lab) is often required or strongly recommended for college-preparatory track.

**AP Chemistry:** College-level course equivalent to two semesters of college general chemistry, emphasizing mathematical modeling and advanced lab techniques.

## Core Chemistry Concepts

### 1. Atomic Structure and Periodicity

**Key Concepts:**
- Subatomic particles (protons, neutrons, electrons)
- Atomic number, mass number, isotopes
- Electron configuration (orbitals, shells, subshells)
- Periodic table organization (groups, periods, trends)
- Periodic trends (atomic radius, ionization energy, electronegativity, electron affinity)

**AP Chemistry Emphasis:**
- Quantum mechanics (orbitals, energy levels)
- Photoelectron spectroscopy (PES)
- Electron configuration and periodicity explanations

---

### 2. Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure

**Key Concepts:**
- Ionic bonding (metal + nonmetal, electron transfer, lattice)
- Covalent bonding (nonmetal + nonmetal, electron sharing)
- Metallic bonding
- Lewis dot structures
- VSEPR theory (molecular geometry)
- Polarity (polar vs. nonpolar molecules)
- Intermolecular forces (hydrogen bonding, dipole-dipole, London dispersion)

**AP Chemistry Emphasis:**
- Hybridization (sp, spÂ², spÂ³)

### 3. Chemical Reactions and Stoichiometry

**Key Concepts:**
- Types of reactions (synthesis, decomposition, single/double replacement, combustion)
- Balancing equations (law of conservation of mass)
- Stoichiometry (mole ratios, limiting reactant, percent yield)
- Mole concept (Avogadro's number, molar mass)

**AP Chemistry Emphasis:**
- Net ionic equations (spectator ions)
- Stoichiometric calculations with multiple steps
- Empirical and molecular formulas
- Percent composition

---

### 4. States of Matter and Solutions

**Key Concepts:**
- States of matter (solid, liquid, gas)
- Phase changes (melting, freezing, boiling, condensation, sublimation, deposition)
- Gas laws (Boyle's, Charles's, Gay-Lussac's, Combined, Ideal Gas Law)
- Solutions (solute, solvent, concentration: molarity, percent, ppm)
- Solubility (factors affecting: temperature, pressure, polarity)
- Colligative properties (boiling point elevation, freezing point depression)

**AP Chemistry Emphasis:**
- Kinetic molecular theory (KMT)
- Deviations from ideal gas behavior
- Vapor pressure and phase diagrams
- Colligative properties calculations

## Quick Reference

**Every HS Chemistry Material Must:**
- Align to NGSS HS-PS1 or state standards (TEKS, etc.)
- Emphasize inquiry and investigation (NGSS practices)
- Include hands-on labs (16 AP labs for AP Chemistry, lab safety)
- Develop quantitative problem-solving skills (stoichiometry, gas laws, equilibrium)
- Use molecular models and representations
- Connect to real-world applications (materials, energy, environment, health)
- Prepare for AP exam (if applicable)
- Support emergent bilinguals (vocabulary, math scaffolding)

**Remember:** Chemistry is the central science, connecting physics and biology, explaining the material world from atoms to ecosystems, and powering innovations in medicine, energy, and materials.
