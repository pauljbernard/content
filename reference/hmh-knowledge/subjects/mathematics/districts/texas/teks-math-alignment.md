# TEKS Math Alignment Guide
**Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills for Mathematics**
**Source:** Texas Education Agency
**Applies To:** K-8 Mathematics (Texas only)

---

## Overview

**Texas uses TEKS (Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills)** for mathematics content standards. All Texas mathematics instructional materials must explicitly align to TEKS Math standards and comply with SBOE Quality Rubric requirements.

**Core Principle:** Every lesson and activity must align to specific TEKS Math standards with clear evidence of alignment, progressive rigor, and support for emergent bilinguals through ELPS.

---

## TEKS Math Standard Format

### Structure:

**Format:** `{Grade}.{Strand}.{Standard Letter}`

**Example:** `5.3A`

**Breakdown:**
- `5` = Grade 5
- `3` = Strand 3 (Number and Operations - Fractions)
- `A` = First standard within strand

## TEKS Math Strands

### All Grades (K-8):

**Strand 1: Mathematical Process Standards**
- Apply to ALL mathematics activities (like CCSS SMPs but integrated)
- Not assessed separately, but embedded in content instruction

**Process Standards:**
- **1A** - Apply mathematics to everyday experiences
- **1B** - Use problem-solving model
- **1C** - Select tools strategically
- **1D** - Communicate mathematical ideas
- **1E** - Create and use representations
- **1F** - Analyze relationships
- **1G** - Display, explain, and justify thinking

---

**Strand 2: Number and Operations**
- Counting (K-2)
- Place value (K-5)
- Comparing and ordering numbers
- Whole number operations

**Strand 3: Number and Operations - Fractions** (Grades 3-5 only)
- Fraction concepts and equivalence
- Operations with fractions

**Strand 4: Number and Operations - Decimals** (Grades 4-5 only)
- Decimal concepts
- Operations with decimals
- Relationship to fractions

**Strand 5: Algebraic Reasoning**
- Patterns and relationships
- Expressions and equations
- Representing situations algebraically

**Strand 6: Geometry and Measurement**
- Geometric figures and properties
- Measurement concepts
- Perimeter, area, volume

**Strand 7: Data Analysis** (Grades 1-5)
- Data collection and representation
- Analysis and interpretation

**Strand 8: Personal Financial Literacy**
- Financial decision-making
- Saving, spending, borrowing

**Strand 2: Number and Operations**

## TEKS vs. CCSS-M Key Differences

### 1. Process Standards Integration

**TEKS Approach:**
```
Every TEKS content standard implicitly includes Process Standards (Strand 1).
Teachers address 1A-1G through content instruction.
```

**CCSS-M Approach:**
```
8 Mathematical Practices (SMPs) are separate.
Lessons explicitly connect activities to specific SMPs.
```

**Implication for Texas:** Process standards are embedded, not called out separately. But SBOE rubric expects evidence of mathematical reasoning, communication, and problem-solving in every lesson.

---

### 2. Financial Literacy Strand

**TEKS Unique Requirement:**
Every grade K-8 includes a Personal Financial Literacy strand.

**Examples:**
- **Grade 3:** `3.8C` - Compare advantages/disadvantages of credit/debit cards
- **Grade 5:** `5.10A` - Define income tax, payroll tax, sales tax, property tax
- **Grade 8:** `8.12E` - Calculate and compare simple vs. compound interest

**Implication:** Texas math curricula must include financial literacy content not present in CCSS-M.

---

### 3. More Granular Standards

**TEKS Example (Grade 5 Fractions):**
```
5.3H - Represent/solve addition/subtraction with fractions (denominators â‰¤ 12)
5.3I - Represent/solve addition/subtraction with mixed numbers (denominators â‰¤ 12)
```

**Difference:** TEKS breaks into more specific expectations; CCSS-M is broader.

## Alignment Documentation

### Required Elements:

**1. TEKS Math Standard(s)**
```
TEKS: 5.3A
Estimate to determine solutions to mathematical and real-world problems
involving addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.
```

**2. Process Standards Addressed**
```
Process Standards (implicit):
- 1B: Use problem-solving model (estimation strategy)
- 1D: Communicate reasoning for estimate
- 1F: Analyze reasonableness of result
```

**3. ELPS Connection** (for EB support)
```
ELPS 3.E: Share estimation strategies in cooperative learning
ELPS 4.C: Develop math vocabulary (estimate, round, benchmark)
```

**4. Evidence of Alignment**
Describe how lesson activities address the standard

## Example Alignment (Grade 5 Fraction Addition):

**Standard:**
```
5.3K
Add and subtract positive rational numbers fluently.
```

**Lesson Alignment:**
- **Spark Your Learning (SYL):** Students explore adding fractions with models
- **Task 1:** Students find common denominators using equivalent fractions
- **Task 2:** Students add fractions with unlike denominators
- **Task 3:** Students solve real-world problems requiring fraction addition
- **Quick Check:** Students demonstrate fluency adding/subtracting fractions

**Process Standards Addressed:**
- **1B:** Use problem-solving model to approach fraction addition
- **1D:** Explain strategies for finding common denominators
- **1E:** Use models, number lines, and equations to represent fraction sums
- **1G:** Justify solution paths and check reasonableness

**ELPS Addressed:**
- **ELPS 2.C:** Learn fraction vocabulary in context (denominator, equivalent, sum)
- **ELPS 3.E:** Share fraction addition strategies in small groups
- **ELPS 4.C:** Use strategic scaffolds for understanding fraction concepts

**Math Language Routines Used:**
- **MLR1:** Stronger and Clearer Each Time (explain fraction addition strategy)
- **MLR2:** Collect and Display (capture student fraction strategies)
- **MLR7:** Compare and Connect (compare different solution paths)

**Progressive Rigor:**
- **Foundational (SYL + Task 1):** Explore with models, DOK 1-2
- **Developing (Task 2):** Apply procedure with scaffolds, DOK 2
- **Advancing (Task 3):** Solve context problems, DOK 2-3
- **Mastery (Practice):** Demonstrate fluency, DOK 2-3

## Alignment Validation Checklist

### Content Alignment:
- [ ] TEKS standard code correct and complete (e.g., 5.3A not just "5.3")
- [ ] Full standard text included or referenced
- [ ] All parts of standard addressed in lesson
- [ ] Grade-level appropriate (no advanced or remedial content unless scaffolding)
- [ ] Financial literacy addressed (if applicable to grade/strand)

### Process Standards:
- [ ] At least 2-3 process standards (1A-1G) implicitly addressed
- [ ] Problem-solving model applied (TEKS 1B)
- [ ] Mathematical communication opportunities provided (TEKS 1D)
- [ ] Multiple representations used (TEKS 1E)

### ELPS Alignment:
- [ ] At least 1-2 ELPS standards explicitly connected
- [ ] Math language development supported (ELPS 1-4)
- [ ] EB scaffolds provided at 3 proficiency levels (Beginning, Intermediate, Advanced)

### Math Language Routines:
- [ ] At least 1 MLR embedded in lesson
- [ ] MLR aligns with TEKS and ELPS focus
- [ ] Time allocated for MLR implementation

### Progressive Rigor:
- [ ] Tasks increase in complexity within lesson (SBOE 4.1b)
- [ ] DOK levels progress from 1-2 to 2-3
- [ ] Scaffolding decreases as lesson progresses
- [ ] Quick Check assesses at appropriate rigor level

### Assessment:
- [ ] Assessment items align to TEKS standard
- [ ] DOK level matches standard expectation
- [ ] Rubric provided for constructed response items
- [ ] Answer key includes TEKS alignment codes

## SBOE Quality Rubric - Math Specific Indicators

### Dimension 4: Building Knowledge (Math Focus)

**4.1 Instructional Design:**
- (a) Lessons build coherently toward mastery of TEKS
- (b) **Questions and tasks progress in complexity** (progressive rigor)
- (c) Materials include spiraled review and practice

**4.2 Practice and Application:**
- (a) Multiple representations of mathematical concepts
- (b) Concrete-Representational-Abstract progression
- (c) Connections to real-world applications

**4.3 Review and Assessment:**
- (a) **Spaced retrieval practice** over time
- (b) Formative and summative assessments aligned to TEKS
- (c) **Interleaved practice** of prior content

**4.4 Differentiation:**
- (a) Scaffolds for struggling learners
- (b) Extensions for advanced learners
- (c) Multiple entry points for diverse learners

---

### Dimension 5: Developing Mathematical Fluency

**5.1 Procedural Fluency:**
- (a) Systematic development of computational fluency
- (b) Practice opportunities leading to automaticity
- (c) **Fluency expectations clearly labeled** (e.g., "Fluency Story")

**5.2 Conceptual Understanding:**
- (a) Conceptual understanding before procedures
- (b) Multiple models and strategies
- (c) **Students evaluate and compare procedures**

**5.3 Problem-Solving:**
- (a) Mathematical problem-solving throughout
- (b) **Multiple solution paths encouraged and compared**
- (c) Students justify and explain reasoning

## TEKS Math Adoption Cycles

**State Adoption:** Texas maintains a state-approved instructional materials list.

## Key Takeaways

### For Content Developers:

1. **TEKS â‰  CCSS-M:** Don't assume CCSS-M alignment translates to TEKS alignment. Standard codes and some content differ.

2. **Financial Literacy is Required:** Every grade K-8 must include personal financial literacy strand.

3. **Process Standards are Embedded:** Unlike CCSS SMPs, TEKS process standards (1A-1G) are implicit in all content instruction.

4. **SBOE Rubric Matters:** Pay special attention to progressive rigor, spaced retrieval, interleaved practice, fluency labeling, and multiple solution paths.

5. **ELPS Integration Essential:** All lessons must support emergent bilinguals through explicit math language development.

6. **MLRs are Your Friend:** Math Language Routines address both TEKS process standards AND ELPS requirements simultaneously.

7. **Reuse Compliance Knowledge:** Texas district compliance files (IPACC, SBOE, ELPS) apply to all subjectsâ€”don't recreate!
