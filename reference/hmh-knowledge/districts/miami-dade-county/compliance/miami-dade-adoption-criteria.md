# Miami-Dade County Public Schools Adoption Criteria
**District:** Miami-Dade County Public Schools (M-DCPS)
**State:** Florida
**Students:** 330,000 (fourth largest US district)
**Applies To:** All K-8 instructional materials

---

## Overview

**M-DCPS** is the fourth largest school district in the US and the largest in Florida, serving 330,000 students across Miami-Dade County. The district is exceptionally diverse with 70%+ Hispanic/Latino students and 90+ languages spoken.

**Key Principle:** Materials must meet Florida state standards AND M-DCPS district-specific criteria.

---

## Relationship to Florida State

### State Standards (Base Requirements):

M-DCPS follows all Florida state standards:
- **Content Standards:** B.E.S.T. (Math and ELA), NGSSS/SSS (Science and Social Studies transitioning to new frameworks)
- **Language Standards:** ESOL/WIDA framework
- **State Assessments:** FAST (Florida Assessment of Student Thinking), SSA (Statewide Science Assessment)

**See:** `/districts/florida/compliance/florida-adoption-criteria.md` for state requirements

---

### M-DCPS District Layer (Additional Requirements):

1. **Bilingual/Multicultural Education:** Largest bilingual program in US
2. **Global Competency:** International focus and world languages
3. **College and Career Pathways:** Strong CTE and dual enrollment
4. **Technology Integration:** 1:1 device initiative
5. **Miami Context:** Local culture, Cuban/Latin American history, immigration

---

## M-DCPS-Specific Requirements

### 1. Bilingual/Multicultural Education (70,000+ ELLs)

**M-DCPS serves 70,000+ English Language Learners** (21% of enrollment)

**Largest Languages:**
- Spanish (90%+ of ELLs)
- Haitian Creole
- Portuguese
- French

**Program Types:**
- Bilingual Education (Spanish/English dual language)
- ESOL (English for Speakers of Other Languages)
- Heritage Language programs
- Dual Language Immersion (expanding)

**Requirements:**
- Robust ESOL scaffolds (5 WIDA levels)
- Spanish/English bilingual materials
- Haitian Creole supports (second largest ELL group)
- Culturally responsive to Cuban, Caribbean, Latin American cultures

**See:** `/districts/miami-dade-county/language/miami-dade-ell-framework.md`

---

### 2. Global Competency and International Focus

**M-DCPS Global Learning Initiative**

**Components:**
- International themes and perspectives
- Global issues integration (climate, migration, economics)
- World language emphasis (Spanish, French, Mandarin, Portuguese)
- Sister school partnerships worldwide
- International Baccalaureate (IB) programs (50+ schools)

**Materials Should:**
- Include international perspectives
- Connect local (Miami) to global contexts
- Feature diverse cultures and countries
- Support world language acquisition
- Address global challenges

---

### 3. Miami/South Florida Context

**Local Connection Requirements:**

**Miami History and Culture:**
- Cuban immigration and exile community
- Caribbean influences (Haiti, Jamaica, Bahamas)
- Latin American connections
- Miami as gateway city
- Multicultural identity

**Geography and Environment:**
- Coastal/marine ecosystems
- Hurricane preparedness and climate
- Everglades and subtropical environment
- Urban planning and growth

**Economics and Careers:**
- International trade and commerce
- Tourism and hospitality
- Technology and innovation (Silicon Beach)
- Healthcare and life sciences

---

### 4. Technology Integration (1:1 Devices)

**M-DCPS Digital Learning:**
- All students have district-issued devices
- Google Workspace for Education
- Canvas LMS
- Digital textbooks and resources

**Materials Must:**
- Work seamlessly on Chromebooks
- Integrate with Canvas LMS
- Include digital-native content (not just PDFs)
- Provide offline options
- Support flipped classroom models

---

### 5. College and Career Pathways

**M-DCPS Focus:** Clear pathways from elementary through career

**K-8 Expectations:**
- Career awareness and exploration
- STEM emphasis
- Dual language skills (career asset)
- 21st-century skills
- Growth mindset and college-going culture

**Materials Should:**
- Connect to careers (especially Miami industries)
- Build foundational skills for secondary pathways
- Include real-world applications
- Feature diverse role models in careers

The district's international focus, IB programs, and Miami's unique cultural context require materials that connect local to global perspectives.
